# Compliance Checklists & Risk Management

*Maintaining Legal Compliance and Protecting Community Assets*

---

Ongoing legal compliance requires systematic attention to tax obligations, regulatory requirements, insurance needs, and risk management across all community activities. Without organized compliance systems, communities risk legal penalties, loss of legal status, liability exposure, and disruption of operations. This guide provides checklists and frameworks for maintaining compliance while protecting community assets and member interests.

## Annual Compliance Schedules

### Nonprofit Organization Annual Compliance

**501(c)(3) Public Charity Annual Requirements:**

**Federal Tax Compliance:**
- [ ] File Form 990 or 990-EZ by 15th day of 5th month after fiscal year end
- [ ] Maintain tax-exempt status through compliance with exempt purpose requirements
- [ ] Document public benefit activities and charitable program implementation
- [ ] Monitor political activity limitations and lobbying expenditure restrictions
- [ ] Annual board resolution confirming tax-exempt purpose and activities

**State and Local Compliance:**
- [ ] File annual state charity registration and financial reports
- [ ] Maintain registered agent and office address with state agencies
- [ ] File any required local business licenses and permits
- [ ] Update state tax exemption certificates and property tax exemptions
- [ ] Monitor state charitable solicitation requirements if engaging in fundraising

**Governance and Documentation:**
- [ ] Conduct annual member and board meetings with proper notice and documentation
- [ ] Maintain corporate records including meeting minutes and governance decisions
- [ ] Update bylaws and governance documents based on community evolution and legal changes
- [ ] Annual conflict of interest policy review and board member disclosure updates
- [ ] Document charitable activities and beneficiaries for tax-exempt status protection

### Cooperative Organization Annual Compliance

**Worker Cooperative Annual Requirements:**

**Federal and State Tax Compliance:**
- [ ] File appropriate tax returns based on cooperative tax election and structure
- [ ] Calculate and distribute patronage refunds to members based on participation
- [ ] Maintain cooperative tax status through compliance with cooperative principles
- [ ] Document cooperative education and member benefits for tax compliance
- [ ] Annual review of cooperative tax election and potential changes

**Cooperative Governance Compliance:**
- [ ] Annual member meeting with required notice and participation procedures
- [ ] Board election and governance accountability to membership
- [ ] Annual cooperative education requirement for members
- [ ] Maintain cooperative principles compliance including democratic governance and member economic participation
- [ ] Document cooperative operations and member benefits for legal status protection

### Limited Liability Company Annual Compliance

**LLC Annual Requirements:**

**State Registration and Reporting:**
- [ ] File annual state LLC registration and pay required fees
- [ ] Maintain registered agent and office address with state agencies
- [ ] Update LLC operating agreement based on community governance evolution
- [ ] Annual member meeting documentation and governance record maintenance

**Federal Tax Compliance:**
- [ ] File appropriate federal tax returns based on LLC tax election
- [ ] Issue Schedule K-1 forms to members reporting income and loss allocation
- [ ] Maintain accurate financial records supporting tax reporting and member distributions
- [ ] Monitor employment tax obligations for member work arrangements
- [ ] Professional tax consultation about alternative currency and cooperative economics tax implications

## Insurance and Risk Management

### Comprehensive Insurance Assessment

**Property and Casualty Insurance:**
Community property and activities require insurance coverage that protects both community assets and individual members from liability.

**Essential Insurance Coverage:**

*Property Insurance:*
- [ ] Buildings and infrastructure coverage including replacement cost protection
- [ ] Personal property coverage for community-owned equipment and supplies
- [ ] Business interruption coverage for community enterprises and revenue sources
- [ ] Additional coverage for alternative building materials and construction methods

*General Liability Insurance:*
- [ ] Public liability coverage for community events and visitor activities
- [ ] Professional liability coverage for members providing services on behalf of community
- [ ] Product liability coverage for community enterprises selling products or services
- [ ] Volunteer accident coverage for community members injured during community work

*Specialized Coverage for Alternative Communities:*
- [ ] Directors and officers liability insurance for community leaders and governance participants
- [ ] Employment practices liability insurance for community work arrangements
- [ ] Cyber liability insurance for community technology and data systems
- [ ] Professional consultation about alternative currency and cooperative economics insurance implications

### Risk Assessment and Management Planning

**Community Risk Evaluation:**
Systematic risk assessment enables communities to prioritize insurance coverage and prevention strategies based on actual community vulnerabilities.

**Risk Categories and Assessment:**

*Operational Risks:*
- Community member injury during work activities or community events
- Property damage from natural disasters, accidents, or equipment failure
- Community enterprise liability including customer injury or product defects
- Financial loss from theft, fraud, or economic disruption

*Governance and Legal Risks:*
- Legal challenges to community governance or decision-making authority
- Member disputes escalating to legal action against community
- Regulatory violations resulting in fines or operational restrictions
- Employment law violations from community work arrangements

*Alternative Currency and Economic Risks:*
- Tax compliance issues from Hearts/Leaves currency operations
- Regulatory challenges to alternative economic arrangements
- Member financial disputes affecting community operations
- Banking and financial service access problems due to alternative economics

### Emergency Legal Response Protocols

**Crisis Legal Response:**
Communities need clear procedures for legal emergencies including regulatory enforcement actions, lawsuits, and member legal issues.

**Legal Emergency Response Procedures:**

*Immediate Response (24-48 hours):*
- [ ] Attorney consultation within 24 hours for any legal notices or enforcement actions
- [ ] Community emergency governance procedures for legal crisis decision-making
- [ ] Documentation preservation and legal privilege protection for sensitive community information
- [ ] Community communication protocols preventing self-incrimination while maintaining transparency

*Community Legal Defense Coordination:*
- [ ] Legal defense fund activation and resource allocation for community legal challenges
- [ ] Professional legal representation coordination with community values and governance participation
- [ ] Community member legal protection and support during legal challenges
- [ ] Media and public relations coordination protecting community reputation during legal challenges

**Member Legal Issues:**
When individual members face legal challenges that could affect community operations.

*Community Support and Protection:*
- [ ] Clear policies about community responsibility for member legal issues versus individual responsibility
- [ ] Legal support fund availability for members facing legal challenges related to community activities
- [ ] Professional consultation about community liability for member actions
- [ ] Community decision-making about continued membership for members with legal problems

Legal compliance and risk management require ongoing professional consultation balanced with community education and self-reliance. Communities should invest in adequate professional legal support while developing internal capacity for understanding and managing routine legal requirements.

The goal is creating legal compliance systems that protect community autonomy while ensuring sustainability through appropriate legal structure and risk management. This requires professional legal consultation, community legal education, and integration of legal requirements with community governance and operational development.

Communities that establish systematic legal compliance often find they have greater freedom to innovate because they have protected themselves from legal vulnerabilities while building relationships with professional legal support and regulatory agencies that understand and respect alternative community approaches.
