# Question Banks & Dialogue Frameworks

*Structured Resources for Vision Development and Community Building*

---

Well-crafted questions can transform superficial discussions into meaningful dialogues that build understanding, generate collective wisdom, and strengthen community bonds. This resource provides comprehensive question libraries and conversation frameworks organized by purpose and community development stage.

## Vision Building and Values Exploration

### Community Vision Development

**Initial Vision Dialogue Framework:**
Use this sequence for communities beginning their vision development process.

**Round 1: Connection and Place (30-45 minutes)**
- What drew you to this place or this community vision?
- When you imagine yourself here in 10 years, what are you doing on a typical day?
- What does this land want to become? (Take time in nature before answering)
- What would make this community a place where your children or future generations would want to live?

**Round 2: Values and Principles (45-60 minutes)**
- What values do you want our community to embody in daily life?
- Describe a time when you experienced the kind of community you want to create here.
- What would someone visiting our community notice about how we treat each other and the land?
- What principles should guide us when we face difficult decisions?

**Round 3: Vision Integration (30-45 minutes)**
- Complete this sentence: "Our community exists so that..."
- What would need to be true for our vision to flourish 50 years from now?
- How do our individual visions connect to create something larger than what any of us could create alone?
- What is our gift to the bioregion and the world?

**Deep Vision Exploration Questions:**
For communities ready to go beyond surface-level visioning:

*Purpose and Mission:*
- What healing does our community offer to the world?
- What aspect of the current system are we most called to transform?
- How does our community vision serve the seventh generation?
- What would we be willing to sacrifice to achieve our vision?

*Cultural and Social Vision:*
- What stories do we want visitors to tell about our community?
- How do we want to be remembered in the bioregional history?
- What traditions and rituals will we create and maintain?
- How will we handle disagreement and conflict in ways that align with our values?

### Values Clarification and Integration

**Core Values Exploration:**

*Individual Values Reflection:*
- What three values are most essential to who you are as a person?
- When have you felt most aligned with your values? What was that experience like?
- What values do you never want to compromise, even under pressure?
- How do your personal values connect with your desire to live in community?

*Collective Values Discovery:*
- What values do we share across our different backgrounds and experiences?
- Where do our individual values create tension or require negotiation?
- What values do we want to be known for in the broader community?
- How do our stated values connect to our daily decisions and actions?

*Values in Action Framework:*
- Economic decisions: How do our values guide how we handle money and resources?
- Governance decisions: What do our values tell us about power and decision-making?
- Relationship decisions: How do our values shape how we treat each other during conflicts?
- Environmental decisions: What do our values require of us as land stewards?

## Community Assessment and Challenge Exploration

### Current Reality Assessment

**Community Health Check-In:**
Regular dialogue framework for assessing community well-being and identifying areas for attention.

**Social and Relationship Health:**
- On a scale of 1-10, how connected do you feel to other community members right now?
- What relationships in our community are thriving? What makes them work well?
- Where do you notice tension or disconnection that we might want to address?
- What support do you need from the community to feel fully engaged and contributing?

**Governance and Decision-Making:**
- How satisfied are you with our current decision-making processes?
- When do you feel most heard and influential in community governance?
- What decisions are we avoiding that we need to address?
- Where do you see gaps between our stated values and our governance practices?

**Economic and Resource Well-being:**
- How sustainable does our current economic model feel to you personally and collectively?
- What resources do we have in abundance? What are we lacking?
- How well are we balancing individual financial needs with community economic goals?
- What economic changes do we need to make in the next year?

**Ecological and Land Relationship:**
- How connected do you feel to this land and bioregion?
- What do you notice about the health of our ecosystem and our impact on it?
- Where are we succeeding as land stewards? Where do we need to improve?
- What does the land need from us that we're not currently providing?

### Challenge and Problem Identification

**Challenge Exploration Framework:**
For addressing specific community problems or difficult decisions.

**Problem Definition and Understanding:**
- How would you describe this challenge in your own words?
- What underlying needs or values are at stake in this situation?
- Who is most affected by this problem? How are they impacted?
- What assumptions might we be making about this situation that we should examine?

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- What do you think are the deeper causes of this challenge?
- How does this problem connect to larger systems or patterns in our community?
- What historical or cultural factors might be contributing to this situation?
- Where do you see this pattern showing up in other aspects of community life?

**Stakeholder Impact Assessment:**
- Who has the most to gain or lose from different approaches to this challenge?
- Whose voices are we hearing clearly in this discussion? Whose might we be missing?
- How does this decision affect different generations in our community?
- What are the impacts on our bioregion and neighboring communities?

## Decision-Making and Planning Dialogues

### Collaborative Decision-Making

**Decision Preparation Framework:**
Use before formal decision-making processes to ensure informed and thoughtful choices.

**Information and Perspective Gathering:**
- What information do we need to make a wise decision about this?
- What different perspectives and viewpoints should we consider?
- What are the potential consequences of different choices?
- What similar decisions have other communities made? What did they learn?

**Values and Criteria Identification:**
- What values should guide our decision-making process?
- What criteria will we use to evaluate different options?
- What outcomes are most important to us in this decision?
- What are we willing to sacrifice and what is non-negotiable?

**Option Generation and Evaluation:**
- What options have we considered? What other possibilities might exist?
- How does each option align with our stated values and criteria?
- What would each option require from us in terms of resources and commitment?
- Which options feel energizing? Which feel draining or concerning?

**Implementation and Follow-Through:**
- What would successful implementation of this decision look like?
- Who needs to be involved in implementation for this decision to succeed?
- How will we know if our decision is working? What will we monitor?
- What support and resources do we need to make this decision successful?

### Strategic Planning and Future Orientation

**Annual Planning and Visioning:**

*Reflection on Previous Year:*
- What are you most proud of from our community's development over the past year?
- What challenges did we face and how did we handle them?
- What did we learn about ourselves as a community?
- Where did we fall short of our intentions and what did that teach us?

*Current Capacity and Resources:*
- What strengths and assets do we have to work with in the coming year?
- Where do we have energy and enthusiasm for new projects or directions?
- What limitations or constraints do we need to work within?
- What support or resources do we need to develop or access?

*Future Direction and Priorities:*
- What feels most important for our community to focus on this year?
- Where do we see the greatest opportunities for positive impact?
- What would we regret not attempting this year?
- How do our annual goals connect to our long-term vision?

## Seasonal and Cyclical Dialogues

### Seasonal Community Reflection

**Spring: New Beginnings and Growth**
- What wants to be born or begin in our community this season?
- Where do you feel called to grow or develop personally within our community context?
- What new relationships or collaborations are you curious about?
- How can we support the new growth happening among community members?

**Summer: Activity and Abundance**
- Where do you see abundance and flourishing in our community right now?
- What community activities and projects are bringing you the most energy and joy?
- How are we sharing our gifts with each other and with the broader community?
- What do we want to celebrate about our community life?

**Fall: Harvest and Gratitude**
- What have we harvested this year in terms of relationships, learning, and community development?
- What are you most grateful for in our shared community life?
- What do we want to preserve and carry forward from this period?
- How have we grown individually and collectively?

**Winter: Rest and Reflection**
- What do you need to release or let go of from this year?
- Where do you need rest and renewal in your community participation?
- What wisdom have we gained that we want to integrate and remember?
- How can we support each other through quieter, more reflective times?

### Life Cycle and Transition Dialogues

**New Member Integration:**
- What hopes and concerns do you bring to joining our community?
- How do your gifts and skills connect with community needs and opportunities?
- What do you need from us to feel welcomed and supported?
- What questions do you have about community life that would help you participate more fully?

**Member Departure and Transition:**
- What has been most meaningful about your time in our community?
- What wisdom or insights do you want to share based on your community experience?
- How can we stay connected and supportive during your transition?
- What do you want us to remember about your contributions to community life?

**Community Milestone Recognition:**
- How has our community changed and developed since [significant milestone]?
- What achievements are we most proud of during this period?
- What challenges have we successfully navigated together?
- What do we want to commit to for the next phase of our development?

## Customizable Dialogue Templates

### Template: Community Challenge Dialogue

**Purpose:** Address specific community problems through inclusive dialogue

**Time:** 2-3 hours

**Process:**
1. **Check-in Round** (20 minutes): How is this challenge affecting you personally?
2. **Problem Exploration** (30 minutes): What are different perspectives on this situation?
3. **Root Cause Analysis** (30 minutes): What underlying factors contribute to this challenge?
4. **Solution Generation** (45 minutes): What approaches might address this situation?
5. **Next Steps Planning** (15 minutes): What are we committed to doing next?

### Template: Vision Alignment Check

**Purpose:** Assess alignment between community actions and stated vision

**Time:** 90 minutes

**Process:**
1. **Vision Review** (15 minutes): Read community vision statement together
2. **Current Reality Assessment** (30 minutes): How does our daily life reflect our vision?
3. **Gap Identification** (30 minutes): Where do you see differences between vision and reality?
4. **Alignment Planning** (15 minutes): What changes would increase alignment?

### Template: Relationship Building Circle

**Purpose:** Deepen understanding and connection between community members

**Time:** 60-90 minutes

**Process:**
1. **Appreciation Round** (20 minutes): Share appreciation for other community members
2. **Challenge Sharing** (30 minutes): Share current personal or community challenges
3. **Support Offers** (20 minutes): Offer specific support to others based on what you heard
4. **Commitment Round** (10 minutes): Commit to specific actions to strengthen community relationships

## Dialogue Evaluation and Learning

### Assessing Dialogue Effectiveness

**Immediate Post-Dialogue Assessment:**
- Did everyone have opportunity to share their perspective?
- Do we have better understanding of different viewpoints on this topic?
- Do we feel more connected as a community after this conversation?
- What insights or wisdom emerged through our dialogue together?

**Long-term Dialogue Impact:**
- How did insights from this dialogue influence subsequent community decisions or actions?
- What did we learn about our community communication patterns and capacity?
- Which questions or approaches generated the most useful insights?
- How can we improve future dialogues based on this experience?

### Question Development and Adaptation

**Creating Community-Specific Questions:**
Adapt generic frameworks to reflect your community's unique context, values, and challenges.

**Question Adaptation Process:**
1. **Context Assessment:** What makes our community situation unique?
2. **Values Integration:** How can questions reflect our specific values and mission?
3. **Language Adaptation:** What language resonates most with our community members?
4. **Cultural Sensitivity:** How can questions honor different cultural backgrounds represented in our community?

**Question Testing and Refinement:**
- Try new questions with small groups before using in large community dialogues
- Ask for feedback about question clarity and relevance after using them
- Modify questions based on what generates most useful conversation and insight
- Document effective question variations for future use

## Advanced Dialogue Frameworks

### Multi-Session Dialogue Series

**Community Vision Development Series (4 sessions):**

*Session 1: Individual Vision and Values*
- Personal visioning and values clarification
- Individual sharing and witness circle
- Common themes identification

*Session 2: Collective Vision Integration*
- Small group vision synthesis
- Large group integration and pattern recognition
- Draft vision statement development

*Session 3: Vision Testing and Refinement*
- Vision statement review and feedback
- Challenge testing: "What would threaten this vision?"
- Refinement and final version development

*Session 4: Implementation and Commitment*
- Action planning for vision manifestation
- Individual and collective commitments
- Success metrics and accountability systems

### Crisis Response Dialogue Framework

**Emergency Community Dialogue Process:**
For addressing urgent community challenges that require rapid collective response.

**Phase 1: Immediate Response (60-90 minutes)**
- Crisis impact assessment: How is this affecting community members?
- Immediate needs identification: What do we need to address right away?
- Resource inventory: What capacity and resources do we have available?
- Short-term action planning: What will we do in the next 24-48 hours?

**Phase 2: Deeper Analysis (90-120 minutes, within 1 week)**
- Root cause exploration: What underlying factors contributed to this crisis?
- System assessment: What community systems or processes need attention?
- Learning integration: What does this crisis teach us about our community?
- Prevention planning: How can we prevent similar crises in the future?

**Phase 3: Integration and Growth (90 minutes, within 2-4 weeks)**
- Community resilience assessment: How did we handle this crisis together?
- Relationship repair: What relationships need attention after crisis stress?
- System improvements: What changes will we make based on crisis learning?
- Celebration and appreciation: How did we show up well for each other?

### Intergenerational and Cross-Cultural Dialogue

**Bridging Differences Framework:**
For dialogues across age, cultural, or other significant differences within community.

**Preparation Phase:**
- Individual reflection on cultural background and communication style
- Learning about other cultures/generations represented in dialogue
- Intention setting for mutual understanding and respect

**Dialogue Structure:**
- Cultural sharing: What do others need to know about your background to understand your perspective?
- Experience exchange: What has shaped your worldview and values?
- Common ground exploration: What do we share despite our different backgrounds?
- Difference appreciation: How do our differences strengthen our community?
- Integration planning: How can we honor all perspectives in community decisions?

## Implementation Guidelines

### Selecting Appropriate Questions and Frameworks

**Matching Process to Purpose:**
- **Relationship Building:** Use personal sharing and appreciation questions
- **Problem Solving:** Use challenge exploration and solution generation frameworks
- **Vision Development:** Use future-oriented and values-based question sequences
- **Decision Making:** Use criteria development and option evaluation questions

**Group Size and Format Considerations:**
- **Large Groups (15+ people):** Use structured rounds and small group breakouts
- **Medium Groups (8-14 people):** Use circle processes and paired sharing
- **Small Groups (3-7 people):** Use open dialogue with minimal structure
- **Individual Preparation:** Use reflection questions before group dialogue

### Integration with Community Governance

**Regular Dialogue Rhythms:**
- Monthly community check-ins using health assessment questions
- Quarterly vision alignment dialogues using planning frameworks
- Annual strategic planning using comprehensive future-orientation questions
- Crisis response dialogues as needed using emergency frameworks

**Documentation and Follow-Through:**
- Capture key insights and commitments from community dialogues
- Create action plans with specific responsibilities and timelines
- Schedule follow-up conversations to assess progress and address new issues
- Integrate dialogue insights into community policies and decision-making processes

These question banks and frameworks provide starting points rather than rigid scripts. The most effective community dialogues adapt these resources to fit specific community context, culture, and needs while maintaining focus on building understanding, strengthening relationships, and advancing shared community goals.

Community dialogue capacity develops through regular practice and reflection. Communities that establish consistent dialogue rhythms often find they have stronger relationships, clearer shared direction, and more effective collaborative decision-making because members develop skills for productive conversation and collective thinking.

The goal is creating community culture where meaningful dialogue becomes natural aspect of community life rather than special process reserved for major decisions or conflicts. This requires ongoing skill development, appropriate question and process selection, and integration of dialogue insights with concrete community development and governance.
