# Community Skills Mapping Module
*Assessment of Human Capital and Social Readiness for BAZ Formation*

---

The Community Skills Mapping Module provides systematic assessment of community human capital, social cohesion, and readiness for collaborative governance. This module combines individual skills assessment with community-level social analysis to identify strengths, gaps, and development priorities.

## Skills Mapping Framework

### Comprehensive Skills Assessment Approach

The module evaluates community readiness across four integrated dimensions:

**Individual Skills and Expertise (40% of readiness score)**
- Technical and practical skills inventory
- Professional experience and qualifications
- Learning capacity and skill development potential
- Teaching and mentorship capabilities
- Innovation and problem-solving abilities

**Leadership and Governance Capacity (25% of readiness score)**
- Facilitation and consensus-building experience
- Conflict resolution and mediation skills
- Democratic process management capacity
- Strategic planning and vision development
- Community organizing and mobilization experience

**Social Cohesion and Relationships (25% of readiness score)**
- Trust levels and relationship quality
- Communication effectiveness and patterns
- Conflict resolution track record
- Mutual support and reciprocity systems
- Shared values and vision alignment

**Commitment and Participation (10% of readiness score)**
- Time and energy availability
- Financial contribution capacity
- Volunteer service willingness
- Long-term community commitment
- Responsibility and leadership taking

## Section 5: Skills Gap Analysis and Development Planning

### Critical Skills Gap Identification

**Essential Skills Requirement Matrix**

The community skills assessment is compared against essential BAZ skills requirements to identify critical gaps:

**High Priority Skills Gaps (Must be addressed before BAZ formation):**
- Governance and facilitation skills for democratic decision-making
- Financial management and alternative economic systems expertise
- Legal compliance and regulatory navigation capacity
- Conflict resolution and community healing skills
- Ecological restoration and sustainable living knowledge

**Medium Priority Skills Gaps (Should be addressed during early BAZ formation):**
- Technical and infrastructure development skills
- Communication and media management capacity
- Grant writing and resource development expertise
- Teaching and knowledge sharing capabilities
- Emergency response and crisis management skills

**Lower Priority Skills Gaps (Can be developed over time):**
- Specialized technical and professional skills
- Advanced cultural and artistic capabilities
- Specialized healing and wellness skills
- Advanced permaculture and ecological design
- Specialized business and economic development

### Skills Development Strategy

**Community Learning and Development Planning**

*Skills Development Prioritization:*
- Which skills gaps pose the greatest risk to successful BAZ formation?
- Which skills can be developed within the community vs. requiring external training?
- Which community members have the capacity and interest to develop priority skills?
- What timeline is realistic for developing essential skills before BAZ formation?
- What resources (time, money, expertise) are needed for skills development?

*Learning and Development Methods:*
- **Internal Skill Sharing:** Community members teaching and mentoring each other
- **External Training and Workshops:** Bringing in external trainers and workshop facilitators
- **Formal Education and Certification:** Community members pursuing formal education and certification
- **Apprenticeship and Mentorship:** Pairing community members with external mentors and experienced practitioners
- **Peer Learning Networks:** Connecting with other communities and practitioners for knowledge sharing

**Individual Development Planning**

*Personal Skills Development Plans:*
For each community member, develop individual development plans including:

- Priority skills for personal development based on interests and community needs
- Learning goals and timeline for skills development
- Preferred learning methods and development approaches
- Resources and support needed for skills development
- Contribution commitments based on skills and interests

*Development Support and Resources:*
- What learning and development support can the community provide to members?
- What external learning resources and opportunities are available?
- How will the community fund and support member skills development?
- What recognition and appreciation will the community provide for skills development?
- How will new skills be integrated into community roles and responsibilities?

## Section 7: Skills Mapping Results and Analysis

### Community Skills Profile Dashboard

**Comprehensive Skills and Readiness Assessment Results**

The Community Skills Mapping generates a comprehensive dashboard displaying:

*Overall Community Readiness Score:*
- **Total Community Readiness Score:** Weighted score from all assessment categories
- **Individual Skills Readiness:** Assessment of community skills capacity against BAZ requirements
- **Social Cohesion Score:** Assessment of community relationship quality and trust levels
- **Leadership Capacity Rating:** Assessment of community leadership distribution and development
- **Commitment and Participation Score:** Assessment of community member commitment and participation levels

*Skills Gap Analysis:*
- **Critical Skills Gaps:** Priority skills gaps requiring immediate attention
- **Skills Development Opportunities:** Community skills that can be developed through internal capacity
- **External Skills Needs:** Skills requiring external training, consultation, or recruitment
- **Skills Teaching Capacity:** Community capacity for internal skills sharing and development
- **Skills Development Timeline:** Recommended timeline for addressing priority skills gaps

### Skills Development Implementation Plan

**Community Skills Development Strategy**

*Priority Development Areas:*
Based on assessment results, the module generates prioritized development recommendations:

1. **Immediate Priority (0-6 months):** Critical skills gaps that must be addressed before BAZ formation
2. **Short-term Priority (6-18 months):** Important skills gaps to address during early BAZ formation
3. **Medium-term Priority (1-3 years):** Skills development to enhance BAZ sustainability and effectiveness
4. **Long-term Priority (3+ years):** Advanced skills development for mature BAZ operations

*Development Implementation Strategy:*
- **Internal Development:** Skills that can be developed through community member training and mentorship
- **External Training:** Skills requiring external workshops, training, or formal education
- **Recruitment Strategy:** Critical skills that may require recruiting new community members
- **Partnership Development:** Skills gaps that can be addressed through partnerships and collaboration
- **Resource Mobilization:** Funding and resources needed for comprehensive skills development

### Community Readiness Recommendations

**Assessment-Based Community Development Planning**

*High Community Readiness (85-100 points):*
- Strong skills base with manageable gaps
- Excellent social cohesion and leadership capacity
- High commitment levels with effective participation
- Ready for BAZ formation with standard preparation
- Focus on advanced skills development and specialization

*Moderate Community Readiness (70-84 points):*
- Good skills foundation with some critical gaps
- Generally strong social cohesion with some development needs
- Moderate to high commitment with some variability
- Ready for BAZ formation with focused skills development
- Emphasis on gap-filling and leadership development

*Conditional Community Readiness (55-69 points):*
- Basic skills present with significant development needs
- Social cohesion adequate but requiring strengthening
- Variable commitment levels requiring clarification and enhancement
- BAZ formation possible with substantial preparation
- Comprehensive skills development and relationship building needed

*Low Community Readiness (Below 55 points):*
- Major skills gaps requiring extensive development
- Social cohesion challenges requiring significant attention
- Commitment levels unclear or insufficient
- Significant preparation required before BAZ formation consideration
- Focus on fundamental community building and skills development

## Using Skills Mapping Results for BAZ Formation

### Integration with BAZ Formation Process

**Skills Assessment Integration with Six Pillars:**

*Pillar-Specific Skills Integration:*
- **Pillar 0 (Inner Ground):** Social cohesion and relationship quality inform inner work and healing priorities
- **Pillar 1 (Spark):** Vision alignment and communication skills guide vision development and consensus building
- **Pillar 2 (Place):** Ecological and land management skills inform bioregional mapping and stewardship planning
- **Pillar 3 (Legal Vessel):** Legal and governance skills guide legal structure development and compliance planning
- **Pillar 4 (Governance):** Facilitation and leadership skills inform governance system design and implementation
- **Pillar 5 (GGF Integration):** Technical and communication skills guide GGF integration and digital commons development
- **Pillar 6 (Economic Sovereignty):** Economic and financial skills guide alternative economic system implementation

*Skills Development Timeline Integration:*
- Skills development activities integrated with BAZ formation timeline
- Priority skills development scheduled before relevant pillar implementation
- Ongoing skills development planned throughout BAZ formation process
- Advanced skills development planned for post-formation community enhancement

The Community Skills Mapping Module provides communities with comprehensive assessment of their human capital readiness for BAZ formation, clear identification of development priorities, and strategic planning for building the skills and social cohesion necessary for successful collaborative community development.
