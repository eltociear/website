# Mental Health Crisis Response Guide
*Individual Crisis Support, Community Trauma Response, and Suicide Prevention*

---

Mental health crises in BAZ communities require specialized responses that balance individual support with community wellbeing while addressing the systemic factors contributing to mental health challenges. This guide provides protocols for preventing, responding to, and recovering from mental health crises while maintaining trauma-informed practices and community values.

## Understanding Mental Health Crises in Community Context

### Individual Mental Health Crisis Types

**Acute Mental Health Episodes**
- Severe depression episodes affecting individual functioning and community participation
- Anxiety or panic disorders affecting individual wellbeing and community relationships
- Psychotic episodes or severe mental health decompensation requiring immediate support
- Manic episodes affecting individual judgment and community safety
- Dissociative episodes or trauma responses affecting individual safety and functioning

**Suicidal Crisis and Self-Harm**
- Suicidal ideation or planning requiring immediate intervention and safety planning
- Self-harm behaviors affecting individual safety and community wellbeing
- Suicide attempts requiring medical intervention and ongoing support
- Chronic suicidal ideation requiring long-term support and safety planning
- Community member suicide affecting community grief and trauma response

**Substance Use Crisis**
- Substance abuse or addiction affecting individual functioning and community relationships
- Substance use overdose requiring immediate medical intervention
- Withdrawal symptoms requiring medical support and community care
- Substance use affecting community safety or resources
- Dual diagnosis mental health and substance use disorders

**Trauma and PTSD Crisis**
- Acute trauma responses from recent traumatic experiences
- PTSD symptoms affecting individual functioning and community participation
- Complex trauma responses affecting relationships and community engagement
- Historical trauma responses affecting individual and community wellbeing
- Collective trauma from community experiences affecting multiple members

### Community Mental Health Challenges

**Community-Wide Trauma and Stress**
- Collective trauma from external attacks or community crises
- Community stress from economic or social challenges affecting multiple members
- Intergenerational trauma affecting community culture and relationships
- Historical oppression trauma affecting community members and culture
- Climate and environmental trauma affecting community wellbeing and future planning

**Community Relationship and Social Crisis**
- Social isolation of community members affecting mental health and community participation
- Community conflict affecting member mental health and social relationships
- Leadership crisis affecting community stability and member security
- Community identity crisis affecting member belonging and purpose
- Social exclusion or marginalization within community affecting member wellbeing

**Community System Stress**
- Economic crisis affecting community member security and mental health
- Governance crisis affecting community member trust and participation
- Infrastructure failure affecting community member safety and wellbeing
- Legal crisis affecting community member security and confidence
- External pressure affecting community member stress and resilience

## Mental Health Crisis Prevention and Early Intervention

### Community Mental Health Infrastructure

**Trauma-Informed Community Culture**
Creating community culture that promotes mental health and prevents crisis through trauma-informed practices:

*Community Trauma Awareness:*
- **Universal Trauma Education:** Community education recognizing that most community members have experienced some form of trauma
- **Trauma-Informed Communication:** Community communication practices that avoid re-traumatization and promote healing
- **Cultural Trauma Acknowledgment:** Recognition of historical and cultural trauma affecting community members
- **Community Healing Practices:** Regular community practices promoting collective healing and resilience
- **Individual Trauma Support:** Community support for individual members processing personal trauma

*Community Mental Health Promotion:*
- **Community Connection and Belonging:** Community practices promoting social connection and belonging for all members
- **Community Purpose and Meaning:** Community practices helping members find purpose and meaning through community participation
- **Community Celebration and Joy:** Regular community celebration and joy practices promoting positive mental health
- **Community Support and Care:** Community practices ensuring all members receive adequate support and care
- **Community Resilience Building:** Community practices building collective and individual resilience

**Community Mental Health Support Systems**

*Peer Support Networks:*
- **Mental Health First Aid Training:** Training of community members in mental health first aid and crisis support
- **Peer Support Groups:** Community peer support groups for members experiencing mental health challenges
- **Buddy System:** Community buddy system pairing members for mutual mental health support
- **Community Listening Circles:** Regular community listening circles for emotional support and connection
- **Community Mental Health Check-ins:** Regular individual and community mental health check-ins

*Professional Mental Health Integration:*
- **Community Mental Health Practitioners:** Integration of mental health professionals who understand and support community values
- **Community Mental Health Consultation:** Regular consultation with mental health professionals for community mental health planning
- **Crisis Mental Health Support:** Access to crisis mental health support for emergency mental health situations
- **Community Mental Health Training:** Training of community members in basic mental health support and intervention
- **Community Mental Health Resources:** Community access to mental health resources including books, workshops, and online resources

### Early Warning and Prevention Systems

**Mental Health Crisis Prevention**

*Individual Risk Factor Monitoring:*
- **Community Member Wellbeing Check-ins:** Regular check-ins with community members about mental health and wellbeing
- **Social Isolation Prevention:** Community practices preventing social isolation and promoting connection
- **Economic Stress Mitigation:** Community support mitigating economic stress affecting member mental health
- **Conflict Resolution Support:** Community conflict resolution preventing relationship stress from escalating mental health issues
- **Life Transition Support:** Community support for members experiencing major life transitions

*Community Stress Mitigation:*
- **Community Crisis Communication:** Clear communication about community challenges reducing anxiety and uncertainty
- **Community Resource Security:** Community resource security reducing financial and material stress
- **Community Governance Stability:** Stable community governance reducing uncertainty and insecurity
- **Community External Relationship Management:** Management of external relationships reducing community stress and pressure
- **Community Future Planning:** Community planning for future providing security and hope

**Early Intervention Protocols**

*Mental Health Early Warning Signs:*
- Changes in community member participation or engagement
- Social withdrawal or isolation from community activities
- Increased conflict or difficulty in community relationships
- Changes in personal care or hygiene affecting health and social relationships
- Expressions of hopelessness or despair about personal or community future

*Early Intervention Response:*
- **Immediate Outreach:** Immediate outreach to community members showing early warning signs
- **Support Offering:** Offering appropriate support including peer support, professional support, and community accommodation
- **Safety Assessment:** Assessment of safety needs for community members experiencing mental health challenges
- **Community Accommodation:** Community accommodation for members experiencing mental health challenges
- **Professional Referral:** Referral to professional mental health support when appropriate and available

## Mental Health Crisis Response Protocols

### Mental Health Crisis Response Team

**Community Mental Health Crisis Council**
Specialized team responsible for coordinating community response to mental health crises:

*Crisis Response Authority:*
- **Crisis Intervention Coordination:** Authority to coordinate immediate crisis intervention and support for community members in mental health crisis
- **Professional Support Coordination:** Authority to coordinate professional mental health support including hospitalization when necessary
- **Community Safety Coordination:** Authority to coordinate community safety measures during mental health crises
- **Community Support Coordination:** Authority to coordinate community support for members in crisis and affected community members
- **Crisis Recovery Coordination:** Authority to coordinate crisis recovery and ongoing support planning

*Trauma-Informed Crisis Response:*
- **Individual Rights and Dignity:** Maintaining individual rights and dignity during crisis response
- **Community Member Safety:** Prioritizing community member safety including both crisis member and community safety
- **Voluntary Support Emphasis:** Emphasizing voluntary support and intervention rather than coercive measures when possible
- **Cultural Sensitivity:** Cultural sensitivity in crisis response respecting individual and community cultural values
- **Community Healing:** Attention to community healing and trauma response during and after individual mental health crises

### Individual Mental Health Crisis Response

**Immediate Crisis Intervention Protocol**

*Crisis Assessment and Safety Planning:*
1. **Crisis Severity Assessment:** Immediate assessment of crisis severity and safety needs for individual and community
2. **Safety Planning:** Development of immediate safety plan for individual in crisis and affected community members
3. **Professional Support Engagement:** Engagement of professional mental health support when appropriate and available
4. **Community Support Mobilization:** Mobilization of appropriate community support for individual in crisis
5. **Crisis Monitoring and Follow-up:** Ongoing crisis monitoring and follow-up planning

*Crisis De-escalation and Support:*
- **Crisis De-escalation Techniques:** Use of appropriate crisis de-escalation techniques by trained community members
- **Individual Crisis Support:** Immediate individual support for person in crisis including emotional support and practical assistance
- **Community Member Protection:** Protection of community members from crisis-related harm or trauma
- **Professional Crisis Intervention:** Professional crisis intervention when community support is insufficient or inappropriate
- **Crisis Transportation and Medical Support:** Transportation and medical support for crisis situations requiring medical intervention

**Suicide Prevention and Intervention**

*Suicide Risk Assessment and Intervention:*
- **Suicide Risk Assessment:** Immediate assessment of suicide risk and development of safety planning
- **Suicide Prevention Planning:** Development of suicide prevention plan including removal of means and constant support
- **Professional Suicide Intervention:** Immediate engagement of professional suicide intervention and support
- **Community Suicide Support:** Community support for person experiencing suicidal crisis including continuous monitoring and care
- **Family and Friend Involvement:** Involvement of family and friends in suicide prevention when appropriate and available

*Community Suicide Response:*
- **Community Suicide Prevention:** Community-wide suicide prevention including education and risk factor reduction
- **Community Suicide Postvention:** Community response following suicide or suicide attempt including grief support and prevention
- **Community Suicide Stigma Reduction:** Community efforts to reduce suicide stigma and promote help-seeking
- **Community Suicide Resource Development:** Development of community suicide prevention and intervention resources
- **Community Suicide Education:** Community education about suicide prevention and mental health support

### Community Trauma Response

**Collective Trauma Response Protocol**

*Community Trauma Assessment and Response:*
- **Community Trauma Assessment:** Assessment of community trauma and impact on community members and functioning
- **Community Trauma Healing:** Community trauma healing practices including ceremonial and cultural approaches
- **Individual Trauma Support:** Individual support for community members experiencing trauma responses
- **Community Resilience Building:** Community resilience building practices supporting recovery from trauma
- **Professional Trauma Support:** Professional trauma support and consultation for community trauma response

*Community Healing and Recovery:*
- **Community Healing Ceremonies:** Community healing ceremonies and rituals supporting collective trauma recovery
- **Community Storytelling and Witness:** Community storytelling and witnessing practices supporting trauma processing
- **Community Meaning-Making:** Community meaning-making and purpose-finding following traumatic experiences
- **Community Support and Care:** Enhanced community support and care during trauma recovery
- **Community Future Planning:** Community planning for future supporting hope and resilience following trauma

**Secondary Trauma Prevention**
- **Caregiver Support:** Support for community members providing crisis and trauma support preventing secondary trauma
- **Community Crisis Worker Training:** Training for community crisis workers including self-care and trauma prevention
- **Community Crisis Worker Support:** Ongoing support for community members involved in crisis response
- **Community Trauma Education:** Community education about secondary trauma and prevention strategies
- **Professional Consultation:** Professional consultation for community trauma response preventing secondary trauma

- **Neurodivergent Mental Health Education:** Community education about neurodivergent mental health and support needs

- **Religious and Spiritual Support:** Integration of religious and spiritual support in mental health crisis response when desired by community members
- **Cultural Mental Health Education:** Community education about cultural approaches to mental health and healing
