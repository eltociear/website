# BAZ Starter Pack: One-Page Roadmap

*The Simplest Path to Your First Bioregional Autonomous Zone*

---

**For communities who want to begin immediately without overwhelm**

This ultra-condensed roadmap helps small or new communities take their first steps toward becoming a BAZ using just three essential actions over 6-12 months. Perfect for groups of 5-20 people who are ready to move from inspiration to action.

---

## **Step 1: Spark the Vision** 
*Foundation: Pillar 1 - Vision & Consensus Building*

### What You'll Do
- **Form a Convening Circle** of 5-10 diverse community members
- **Host a Community Dialogue** using the four core questions:
  - What makes this place unique and precious to us?
  - What does a thriving future look like for our grandchildren?
  - What are our deepest community challenges right now?
  - What values and principles should guide our decisions?
- **Draft a Shared Vision Statement** that captures your collective dream

### Your Toolkit
**Community Dialogue Kit**: Facilitator questions, icebreakers, conflict navigation tools, vision statement templates

### Investment Required
- **Budget:** $200-500 (meeting space, materials, refreshments)
- **Time:** 1-2 months, 2-4 community gatherings
- **People:** 5-10 committed core members

### Success Marker
‚úÖ **A clear vision statement that 80% of your group enthusiastically supports**

## **Step 2: Map Your Place**
*Foundation: Pillar 2 - Defining the Bioregion*

### What You'll Do
- **Create a basic watershed map** using free online tools and local knowledge
- **Honor Indigenous territories** through respectful research and outreach (FPIC 2.0)
- **Identify key landmarks** - ecological, cultural, and sacred sites
- **Define your initial zone** - the specific area you'll focus on first

### Your Toolkit
**Bioregional Mapping for Beginners**: Simple GIS tutorials, Indigenous consultation protocols, cultural mapping techniques

### Investment Required
- **Budget:** $500-1,000 (mapping software, consultation fees, workshop materials)
- **Time:** 2-3 months, 4-6 mapping workshops
- **People:** 3-5 members interested in ecology and place-based knowledge

### Success Marker
‚úÖ **A simple but accurate map showing your watershed, key features, and cultural relationships**

---

## **Step 3: Start Small**
*Foundation: Pillar 6 - Economic Sovereignty*

### What You'll Do
- **Launch a pilot project** that embodies your values in action:
  - Community food hub (bulk buying, preservation, seed swaps)
  - Care network (childcare co-op, elder support, skill sharing)  
  - Restoration project (stream cleanup, native plantings, habitat work)
- **Test AUBI Layer 1 payments** with 5-10 volunteer households
- **Create a simple community treasury** to track shared resources and payments

### Your Toolkit
**First 100 Days with AUBI**: Treasury templates, payment guides, legal compliance basics, troubleshooting support

### Investment Required
- **Budget:** $1,000-5,000 (pilot project costs, initial AUBI payments, treasury setup)
- **Time:** 3-6 months, ongoing coordination and monthly gatherings
- **People:** Full founding group plus interested community members (10-20 people)

### Success Marker
‚úÖ **Demonstrable proof that your vision translates into tangible action improving people's lives**

## **Emergency Support: When You Get Stuck**

**Problem:** Can't find 5-10 committed people  
**Solution:** Start with 3, focus on relationship building before projects

**Problem:** Limited budget  
**Solution:** Begin with free tools, volunteer-led workshops, and crowdfunding

**Problem:** Legal concerns about alternative currencies  
**Solution:** Start with time-banking and skill-sharing before monetary systems

**Problem:** Resistance from local authorities  
**Solution:** Frame early work as community organizing, not governance challenges
