# First 90 Days Playbook: From Assessment to Implementation

> *"The journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step, but the direction of that step determines the destination."*
> — Ancient wisdom adapted for governance transformation

**Purpose:** This playbook provides a week-by-week action guide for communities beginning their GGF implementation journey. Whether you've just completed the [Context Assessment](../assessment-rubric) or are ready to pilot your first tools, this guide transforms vision into concrete steps while honoring cultural wisdom and community pace.

**Who This Is For:**
- **New GGF Communities**: Starting their governance transformation journey
- **Implementation Teams**: Needing structured action steps
- **Cultural Leaders**: Integrating traditional governance with GGF frameworks
- **Crisis Responders**: Establishing governance quickly but thoughtfully

## **Before You Begin: Essential Foundations**

### **Prerequisites Checklist**
- [ ] **Community Commitment**: At least 3-5 dedicated people willing to meet weekly
- [ ] **Cultural Grounding**: Connection to local wisdom traditions and protocols
- [ ] **Basic Resources**: Meeting space (physical or digital), communication method
- [ ] **Context Assessment**: Completed the [GGF Context Assessment Rubric](../assessment-rubric)
- [ ] **Tool Stack Selection**: Identified your recommended implementation pathway

### **Cultural Integration Reminder**
Every step in this playbook must be adapted to honor your community's cultural traditions and decision-making processes. The timeline suggestions are flexible—some communities may need months where others need days. **Your cultural wisdom takes precedence over our recommendations.**

## **Days 1-7: Foundation Week**
*"Establishing Sacred Space for Transformation"*

### **Day 1-2: Community Blessing & Intention Setting**

**Action**: Host a community gathering using your traditional opening protocols

**Tasks**:
- [ ] Gather core implementation team (3-8 people recommended)
- [ ] Share the GGF vision using your community's storytelling traditions
- [ ] Conduct opening ceremony appropriate to your culture
- [ ] Establish community intention for governance transformation
- [ ] Create group agreements for working together

**Cultural Adaptation Notes**:
- Indigenous communities: Include elder blessing and land acknowledgment
- Religious communities: Integrate prayer or spiritual practices
- Secular communities: Use values-based intention setting
- Youth-led groups: Include vision sharing and peer agreements

**Output**: Written community intention statement and group agreements

### **Day 3-4: Assessment Review & Tool Selection**

**Action**: Deep dive into your Context Assessment results

**Tasks**:
- [ ] Review your assessment scores together as a community
- [ ] Discuss recommended Tool Stack and Journey pathway
- [ ] Identify any cultural conflicts with recommended tools
- [ ] Select 1-2 specific tools for initial pilot testing
- [ ] Plan cultural adaptations needed for selected tools

**Quick Win Opportunity**: Choose one simple tool you can implement within 2 weeks

**Documentation**: Create a simple "Tool Selection Record" noting:
- Chosen tools and reasons
- Cultural adaptations needed
- Expected timeline
- Success metrics that matter to your community

### **Day 5-7: Resource Mapping & Support Network**

**Action**: Assess your implementation capacity and build support

**Tasks**:
- [ ] Map available resources (people, time, funding, space)
- [ ] Identify skill gaps and training needs
- [ ] Connect with other GGF communities in your region
- [ ] Research local allies and potential resistance
- [ ] Create initial communication strategy for broader community

**Milestone**: End of Week 1 celebration acknowledging your commitment to transformation

---

## **Days 8-21: Preparation Phase**
*"Gathering Tools and Building Capacity"*

### **Week 2: Tool Preparation & Cultural Integration**

**Focus**: Adapting selected tools to your cultural context

**Days 8-10: Tool Study & Adaptation**
- [ ] Study your selected tools thoroughly using the [Tool Database](../tool-database)
- [ ] Identify specific cultural adaptations needed
- [ ] Consult with cultural leaders or elders for guidance
- [ ] Create simplified versions for initial testing

**Days 11-14: Capacity Building**
- [ ] Train implementation team on selected tools
- [ ] Practice facilitation and cultural integration
- [ ] Develop backup plans for common challenges
- [ ] Create materials in appropriate languages and formats

### **Week 3: Community Preparation & Stakeholder Engagement**

**Focus**: Preparing your broader community for tool implementation

**Days 15-17: Stakeholder Mapping**
- [ ] Identify all community members who should be involved
- [ ] Plan appropriate invitation and engagement strategies
- [ ] Address concerns or resistance proactively
- [ ] Ensure inclusive participation across age, gender, and other differences

**Days 18-21: Pilot Preparation**
- [ ] Schedule your first tool pilot session
- [ ] Prepare meeting space and materials
- [ ] Create feedback collection methods
- [ ] Plan documentation and learning capture

**Quick Win Achievement**: Successfully engage broader community in upcoming pilot

---

## **Days 22-49: Implementation Phase**
*"Taking Action and Learning Together"*

### **Week 4-5: First Tool Pilot**

**Focus**: Implementing your first selected tool with community feedback

**Days 22-24: Pilot Launch**
- [ ] Host first tool implementation session
- [ ] Use cultural protocols for opening and decision-making
- [ ] Document what works and what needs adjustment
- [ ] Collect immediate feedback from participants

**Days 25-28: Reflection & Adaptation**
- [ ] Gather implementation team for debrief session
- [ ] Review feedback and identify necessary adaptations
- [ ] Consult cultural advisors on any arising issues
- [ ] Plan improvements for next implementation

**Days 29-35: Refinement & Second Implementation**
- [ ] Implement adapted version of tool
- [ ] Continue feedback collection and documentation
- [ ] Begin planning integration with other community processes
- [ ] Assess readiness for additional tools

### **Week 6-7: Scaling & Integration**

**Focus**: Expanding successful practices and preparing for ongoing implementation

**Days 36-42: Community Integration**
- [ ] Integrate successful tools into regular community practices
- [ ] Begin training additional community facilitators
- [ ] Document your cultural adaptations for sharing
- [ ] Plan connection with other GGF frameworks if appropriate

**Days 43-49: Network Building**
- [ ] Share your experience with other GGF communities
- [ ] Contribute learnings to the [GGF Knowledge Commons](../monitoring-evaluation#peer-learning)
- [ ] Identify opportunities for inter-community collaboration
- [ ] Plan participation in regional GGF networks

---

## **Days 50-90: Integration & Evolution Phase**
*"Embedding New Practices and Planning Ahead"*

### **Week 8-10: Deepening Practice**

**Focus**: Making new governance practices sustainable and effective

**Days 50-56: Practice Refinement**
- [ ] Evaluate effectiveness of implemented tools after 4+ weeks of use
- [ ] Make final cultural adaptations based on sustained practice
- [ ] Train additional community members as facilitators
- [ ] Document your unique cultural innovations

**Days 57-63: Sustainability Planning**
- [ ] Establish regular schedules for ongoing tool use
- [ ] Create succession planning for key roles
- [ ] Integrate tools with existing community systems
- [ ] Plan funding or resource sustainability

**Days 64-70: Impact Assessment**
- [ ] Conduct formal evaluation using culturally appropriate methods
- [ ] Gather stories and qualitative feedback
- [ ] Measure against your community's success indicators
- [ ] Document lessons learned and best practices

### **Week 11-13: Future Planning & Knowledge Sharing**

**Focus**: Planning continued growth and contributing to collective learning

**Days 71-77: Expansion Planning**
- [ ] Assess readiness for additional tools or frameworks
- [ ] Plan connections to economic frameworks (AUBI) if appropriate
- [ ] Consider crisis response capacity development
- [ ] Evaluate regional coordination opportunities

**Days 78-84: Knowledge Contribution**
- [ ] Complete [Pilot Feedback Template](../pilot-feedback-template) for each tool tested
- [ ] Share cultural adaptations with GGF community
- [ ] Contribute to [Success Pattern Library](../appendices/success-patterns)
- [ ] Mentor other communities beginning their journey

**Days 85-90: Celebration & Commitment**
- [ ] Host community celebration of 90-day achievements
- [ ] Renew commitment to continued governance transformation
- [ ] Plan next 90-day implementation cycle
- [ ] Connect with broader GGF movement for mutual support

## **Weekly Rhythm & Practices**

### **Recommended Weekly Structure**

**Monday**: Planning and preparation
**Wednesday**: Community implementation or training
**Friday**: Reflection and adaptation
**Weekend**: Rest and cultural connection

### **Essential Weekly Practices**

1. **Community Check-ins**: Brief gatherings to maintain connection and momentum
2. **Cultural Grounding**: Regular connection to traditions and values
3. **Progress Documentation**: Simple record-keeping of activities and learnings
4. **Peer Support**: Connection with other implementing communities
5. **Rest and Reflection**: Sustainable pace that honors human needs

## **Common Challenges & Solutions**

### **Challenge: Cultural Conflicts**
**Symptoms**: Tools feel foreign or inappropriate to cultural traditions
**Solutions**:
- Return to traditional governance methods as foundation
- Adapt tools rather than adopting them wholesale
- Consult with cultural elders or wisdom keepers
- Document new hybrid approaches for other communities

### **Challenge: Participation Fatigue**
**Symptoms**: Decreasing attendance, lack of engagement
**Solutions**:
- Reduce meeting frequency but increase preparation
- Rotate facilitation and responsibility
- Connect to concrete community benefits and wins
- Integrate fun, celebration, and cultural activities

### **Challenge: Resource Constraints**
**Symptoms**: Lack of time, money, or people for implementation
**Solutions**:
- Start smaller with fewer tools and shorter time commitments
- Integrate with existing community activities rather than adding
- Seek support from [GGF Peer Learning Networks](../monitoring-evaluation#peer-learning)
- Focus on tools that require minimal external resources

### **Challenge: External Resistance**
**Symptoms**: Opposition from existing authorities or institutions
**Solutions**:
- Build alliances with sympathetic individuals in existing systems
- Start with non-threatening tools that improve current processes
- Document clear benefits and positive outcomes
- Connect with regional networks for mutual support

## **Measuring Success: Your Community's Way**

### **Quantitative Indicators**
- **Participation**: Number of community members actively engaged
- **Frequency**: Regularity of tool use and community gatherings
- **Decisions**: Number of decisions made using new governance tools
- **Adaptations**: Cultural innovations created and documented

### **Qualitative Indicators**
- **Trust**: Increased community cohesion and mutual trust
- **Effectiveness**: Better decision-making and conflict resolution
- **Cultural Integrity**: Strengthened connection to traditions and values
- **Innovation**: New approaches that blend traditional and contemporary wisdom

### **Community-Defined Success**
Most importantly, **your community defines what success means** based on your values, needs, and vision. Common community-defined indicators include:
- Decisions that everyone can live with
- Increased youth and elder participation
- Better stewardship of local resources
- Stronger connections to other communities
- Enhanced cultural pride and practice

## **90-Day Completion Celebration**

At the end of your first 90 days, take time to celebrate what you've accomplished:

### **Community Celebration Ritual**
Create a celebration that honors both your cultural traditions and your governance transformation achievements. This might include:
- **Storytelling**: Share stories of challenges overcome and wisdom gained
- **Recognition**: Honor individuals who contributed unique gifts
- **Gratitude**: Express appreciation for ancestors and future generations
- **Commitment**: Renew dedication to continued transformation
- **Connection**: Strengthen bonds within community and with broader movement

### **Knowledge Sharing Gift**
Complete your 90-day cycle by giving back to the global community:
- Submit your adaptations to the [GGF Knowledge Commons](../monitoring-evaluation#knowledge-commons)
- Mentor another community beginning their journey
- Contribute to regional learning networks
- Share your cultural innovations for others to adapt

### **Setting Intentions for Next Phase**
Use your 90-day experience to plan your next implementation cycle:
- What tools or frameworks call to you now?
- How has your community grown and what new capacities do you have?
- What regional or global connections want to develop?
- How can you contribute to the broader GGF movement?
