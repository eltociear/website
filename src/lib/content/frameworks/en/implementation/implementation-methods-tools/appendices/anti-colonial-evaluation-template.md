# Anti-Colonial Evaluation Template
*Systematic Assessment for Decolonization and Cultural Integrity*

> *"Decolonization is not a metaphor. It requires the repatriation of Indigenous land and life. Any framework, tool, or system that does not actively advance Indigenous sovereignty is complicit in the continuation of colonial violence."*  
> ‚Äî Tuck & Yang, "Decolonization is Not a Metaphor"

## Template Overview

The Anti-Colonial Evaluation Template provides systematic methodology for assessing whether governance tools, frameworks, and institutions genuinely serve decolonization or inadvertently reproduce colonial patterns of domination. This template ensures that all GGF implementations strengthen Indigenous sovereignty, traditional governance, and cultural integrity rather than co-opting Indigenous wisdom for settler benefit.

**Essential for evaluating:**
- All GGF tools and frameworks before implementation
- Existing governance institutions and systems seeking decolonization
- Partnerships and collaborations involving Indigenous communities
- Technology and economic systems affecting traditional territories
- Any initiative claiming to serve Indigenous communities or traditional knowledge

**Foundational Principle:** True decolonization requires the dismantling of colonial structures and the restoration of Indigenous sovereignty over traditional territories, governance systems, and cultural knowledge. This template measures progress toward these fundamental transformations.

## Red Flags: Colonial Patterns to Identify and Address

### **Institutional Red Flags** üö©

**Colonial Governance Patterns:**
- **Consultation without Authority**: Indigenous communities consulted but not given decision-making power
- **Token Representation**: Minimal Indigenous participation without meaningful authority or influence
- **Cultural Appropriation**: Indigenous symbols, practices, or knowledge used without permission or appropriate attribution
- **Forced Integration**: Indigenous communities required to conform to non-Indigenous systems and structures
- **Economic Extraction**: Resources or benefits flowing from Indigenous communities to external institutions

**Bureaucratic Colonialism:**
- **Complex Requirements**: Bureaucratic barriers preventing Indigenous participation or access
- **External Standards**: Indigenous communities required to meet non-Indigenous criteria for legitimacy or participation
- **Administrative Control**: Non-Indigenous institutions controlling Indigenous community programs or initiatives
- **Reporting Requirements**: Indigenous communities required to report to non-Indigenous authorities using non-Indigenous criteria
- **Conditional Support**: Indigenous community support contingent on conforming to non-Indigenous expectations

### **Cultural Red Flags** üö©

**Knowledge Appropriation Patterns:**
- **Academic Extraction**: Researchers taking traditional knowledge without community control or benefit-sharing
- **Commercial Appropriation**: Traditional knowledge used for profit without community consent or compensation
- **Spiritual Appropriation**: Traditional spiritual practices adopted without cultural authority or appropriate protocols
- **Cultural Representation**: Non-Indigenous people speaking for or representing Indigenous communities without appropriate authority
- **Sacred Knowledge Violation**: Sacred knowledge shared inappropriately or used without traditional protocols

**Cultural Assimilation Pressure:**
- **Language Suppression**: Indigenous languages discouraged or not supported in governance or education
- **Cultural Practice Restriction**: Traditional practices discouraged or regulated by non-Indigenous authorities
- **Forced Modernization**: Indigenous communities pressured to abandon traditional practices for contemporary alternatives
- **Cultural Translation Requirements**: Traditional knowledge required to be translated into non-Indigenous frameworks for legitimacy
- **Identity Policing**: External authorities determining Indigenous identity or cultural authenticity

### **Economic Red Flags** üö©

**Economic Exploitation Patterns:**
- **Resource Extraction**: Traditional territories exploited for resources without community control or benefit
- **Poverty Tourism**: Indigenous communities used for non-Indigenous education or inspiration without reciprocal benefit
- **Dependency Creation**: Indigenous communities made dependent on non-Indigenous institutions for resources or support
- **Economic Assimilation**: Traditional economic systems undermined in favor of capitalist market participation
- **Conditional Economics**: Economic support contingent on abandoning traditional values or practices

**Development Colonialism:**
- **Imposed Development**: Development projects imposed on Indigenous communities without community control
- **Non-Indigenous Benefit**: Development benefiting non-Indigenous communities more than Indigenous communities
- **Cultural Destruction**: Development destroying traditional territories, sacred sites, or cultural practices
- **Economic Displacement**: Development displacing traditional economic activities or community self-sufficiency
- **Unsustainable Practice**: Development promoting practices that harm traditional territories or seven-generation sustainability
