# Glossary of Implementation Terms
*Definitions for Global Governance Framework Implementation*

> *"Words carry the spirit of our intentions. How we define concepts shapes how we relate to each other and the world. These definitions honor traditional wisdom while building bridges for contemporary coordination."*

## Purpose and Approach

This glossary provides definitions for terms used throughout the Implementation Methods & Tools Framework and broader Global Governance Framework. Definitions prioritize Indigenous concepts and traditional knowledge while ensuring accessibility for diverse communities implementing GGF tools.

**Guiding Principles for Definitions:**
- **Indigenous Wisdom First**: Traditional knowledge and Indigenous concepts lead definitions
- **Cultural Respect**: No appropriation or misrepresentation of sacred or protected knowledge
- **Living Language**: Definitions evolve through community input and traditional guidance
- **Accessibility**: Clear explanations without academic jargon or colonial terminology
- **Multiple Traditions**: Recognition of diverse cultural understandings of concepts

---

## A

**Adaptive Universal Basic Income (AUBI)** | *Economics*  
A regenerative economic system providing unconditional baseline support through hybrid fiat currency and Hearts, with additional Leaves recognition for ecological stewardship. Based on Traditional Indigenous gift economies and Right Relationship principles, AUBI recognizes care work, cultural preservation, and ecological restoration as valuable economic contributions. See: Hearts, Leaves, Love Ledger, Right Relationship.

**Anti-Colonial Evaluation** | *Assessment*  
Systematic assessment process determining whether governance tools, frameworks, or institutions genuinely advance Indigenous sovereignty and decolonization or reproduce colonial patterns of domination. Evaluation criteria include territorial sovereignty, traditional knowledge protection, cultural revitalization, reparative justice, and settler accountability.

**Aurora Accord** | *Technology Governance*  
Global framework governing artificial intelligence development and deployment according to Indigenous ethical principles and Traditional Ecological Knowledge. Ensures AI serves traditional wisdom rather than replacing Indigenous knowledge systems. Requires Indigenous consent for AI applications affecting traditional territories.

---

## B

**Bioregional Autonomous Zone (BAZ)** | *Territory*  
Self-governing territory organized around watersheds, ecosystems, and traditional boundaries rather than colonial administrative lines. BAZs exercise sovereignty over traditional territories under Indigenous authority, implementing regenerative economics and traditional governance while coordinating with other communities for ecosystem protection.

**Bioregional Mapping** | *Territory*  
Process of defining governance territories based on ecological relationships, traditional territories, and natural systems. Uses Traditional Ecological Knowledge to identify watershed boundaries, species migration routes, and cultural landscapes as foundations for community governance jurisdiction.

---

## C

**Care Work** | *Economics*  
Traditional and contemporary activities supporting community wellbeing including childcare, elder support, community healing, conflict resolution, and mutual aid. In AUBI system, care work receives Hearts recognition as valuable economic contribution essential for community flourishing.

**Ceremonial Governance** | *Traditional Practice*  
Decision-making processes emerging through traditional ritual, spiritual practice, and sacred relationship with land. Represents evolution of formal political structures toward organic stewardship systems integrated with spiritual practice and ecological relationship.

**Citizen Assembly** | *Participatory Democracy*  
Deliberative democracy process bringing community members together for collective decision-making on complex issues. In GGF implementation, assemblies operate under Indigenous co-governance with traditional consensus methods integrated with contemporary deliberative techniques.

**Community Weaver** | *Implementation Role*  
Cultural bridge builder supporting community members in navigating AUBI and other GGF systems. Community Weavers provide traditional economic context, system navigation support, and cultural translation while protecting traditional knowledge protocols.

**Consensus-Building** | *Decision-Making*  
Traditional and contemporary methods for achieving community agreement on decisions. Traditional consensus emphasizes relationship and community healing over efficiency, often involving multiple rounds of discussion, elder guidance, and spiritual consultation.

**Constitutional Framework** | *Legal Structure*  
Legal foundation provided by Treaty for Our Only Home enabling democratic participation, Indigenous sovereignty, environmental stewardship, and crisis coordination. All GGF institutions align with constitutional requirements while maintaining community autonomy.

**Context Assessment Rubric** | *Implementation*  
Interactive diagnostic tool evaluating community governance maturity, cultural context, resource availability, and readiness for GGF tool implementation. Provides personalized recommendations for tools and implementation pathways based on community assessment.

**Crisis Response** | *Emergency Governance*  
Rapid governance activation protocols for emergency situations requiring immediate coordination. Integrates traditional emergency leadership with contemporary crisis management while maintaining democratic accountability and cultural integrity.

**Cultural & Ancestral Wisdom Council (CAWC)** | *Institution*  
Meta-governance council providing cultural preservation standards and ancestral wisdom integration across all GGF frameworks. Ensures traditional knowledge protection and Indigenous sovereignty guidance for global coordination efforts.

**Cultural Innovation** | *Traditional Development*  
Traditional knowledge systems adapting to contemporary challenges under Indigenous authority. Includes traditional technology integration, contemporary cultural expression, and traditional governance adaptation while maintaining cultural integrity.

**Cultural Protocol** | *Traditional Practice*  
Traditional methods, timing, and requirements for appropriate cultural participation and knowledge sharing. Includes ceremonial requirements, traditional authority consultation, seasonal considerations, and sacred knowledge protection protocols.

---

## D

**Data Sovereignty** | *Technology*  
Community control over data collection, storage, use, and sharing affecting traditional territories and cultural knowledge. Indigenous data sovereignty ensures traditional knowledge remains under community authority with appropriate consent and benefit-sharing protocols.

**Decolonization** | *Transformation Process*  
Systematic dismantling of colonial structures and restoration of Indigenous sovereignty over traditional territories, governance systems, and cultural knowledge. Requires material changes including land return, institutional transformation, and reparative justice rather than symbolic acknowledgment.

**Democratic Legitimacy** | *Governance*  
Community-based authority for governance systems derived from genuine consent, participation, and accountability rather than external imposition. In GGF context, democratic legitimacy requires alignment with traditional governance and Indigenous sovereignty.

**Digital Justice Tribunal** | *Institution*  
International legal body with jurisdiction over major global harms including ecocide, digital rights violations, and Traditional Knowledge appropriation. Enforces Treaty provisions with authority to order corporate reparations and institutional accountability.

---

## E

**Earth Council (Kawsay Pacha)** | *Institution*  
Senior moral and spiritual authority within Planetary Health Council system providing Traditional Knowledge guidance and Indigenous sovereignty protection across all GGF frameworks. Operates under Indigenous sovereignty principles with traditional consensus decision-making.

**Ecological Stewardship** | *Environmental Practice*  
Traditional and contemporary practices maintaining ecological balance and supporting ecosystem health. Includes traditional land management, species protection, habitat restoration, and sustainable resource use recognized through Leaves currency in AUBI system.

**Emergency Legitimacy** | *Crisis Governance*  
Time-limited governance authority during crisis situations requiring rapid coordination. Operates under traditional authority guidance with automatic expiration and community accountability requirements to prevent authoritarian capture.

---

## F

**Framework Health Audit** | *Assessment*  
Annual review of Implementation Methods & Tools Framework assessing adaptability, scalability, and alignment with emerging challenges. Conducted by independent GGF entity with community input and traditional knowledge keeper guidance.

**Free, Prior, and Informed Consent (FPIC 2.0)** | *Cultural Protocol*  
Enhanced Indigenous consent protocols respecting traditional decision-making timeframes and processes. Requires ongoing consent verification with community authority to modify or withdraw participation based on traditional protocols and cultural requirements.

---

## G

**GGF Failure Library** | *Learning System*  
Repository of anonymized implementation failures and challenges providing systematic learning for community adaptation and innovation. Managed by Knowledge Commons Ombudsperson with community authority over failure documentation and learning sharing.

**Global Commons Fund** | *Economic Mechanism*  
Fund for planetary public goods financed by global taxes and managed through Treaty implementation. Supports BAZ development, Indigenous sovereignty, ecological restoration, and community economic development under democratic oversight.

**Governance Upgrade Kit** | *Tool*  
Library of proven templates for integrating new voices and upgrading decision-making architecture within existing institutions. Includes traditional governance integration, participatory democracy enhancement, and cultural protocol adaptation.

---

## H

**Hearts** | *Currency*  
Care economy currency recognizing community contributions including caregiving, cultural preservation, community building, and mutual aid. Distributed through Love Ledger based on community validation of care work and traditional economic activities supporting community wellbeing.

---

## I

**Implementation Journey** | *Methodology*  
Curated pathways for GGF tool implementation based on community readiness and goals. Journeys include Getting Started, Crisis Response, Building Capacity, Deepening Practice, and Evolution & Transition with appropriate tool combinations.

**Indigenous Co-Governance** | *Governance Model*  
Shared decision-making authority between Indigenous communities and other governance bodies with Indigenous sovereignty and traditional authority recognized as foundational. Requires traditional knowledge keeper involvement and cultural protocol integration.

**Indigenous Sovereignty** | *Political Principle*  
Indigenous communities' inherent right to self-determination, territorial control, cultural authority, and governance autonomy without external interference. Foundational principle for all GGF implementation requiring traditional authority recognition and support.

**Institution Chartering** | *Legal Process*  
Creation of new governance institutions aligned with Treaty constitutional framework while respecting Indigenous sovereignty and traditional governance systems. Includes constitutional compliance, cultural integration, and community consent requirements.

---

## J

**Journey-Based Implementation** | *Methodology*  
Organization of tools and resources into curated pathways based on community context and goals rather than overwhelming communities with comprehensive tool database. Enables appropriate tool selection and phased implementation approach.

---

## K

**Knowledge Commons Ombudsperson** | *Institution Role*  
Independent oversight position managing tool evolution governance, failure library documentation, and learning integration across GGF implementation. Ensures community authority over knowledge sharing and traditional knowledge protection.

---

## L

**Language Justice** | *Cultural Protocol*  
Multi-language operation and communication ensuring Indigenous and community languages are prioritized over colonial languages. Includes skilled interpretation, cultural translation, and community-controlled language preservation support.

**Leaves** | *Currency*  
Ecological economy currency recognizing environmental stewardship including traditional land management, ecosystem restoration, species protection, and sustainable resource practices. Distributed through Love Ledger based on community validation of ecological contributions.

**Love Ledger** | *Technology Platform*  
Community-controlled blockchain system tracking Hearts and Leaves distribution based on community contribution validation. Maintains data sovereignty with traditional knowledge protection while enabling transparent economic coordination across communities.

**Love, Meaning, and Connection Index (LMCI)** | *Assessment Metric*  
Wellbeing measurement system replacing GDP with indicators of community relationship health, cultural vitality, and ecological connection. Based on traditional knowledge about human flourishing and seven-generation thinking.

---

## M

**Meta-Governance** | *Coordination Framework*  
Systems enabling diverse governance frameworks to coordinate effectively without sacrificing autonomy or cultural distinctiveness. Provides coordination protocols while respecting Indigenous sovereignty and traditional governance authority.

**Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning (MEL)** | *Assessment System*  
Continuous assessment and adaptation protocols ensuring tools serve community needs and traditional values. Includes reflexive cycle protocols, effectiveness ratings, failure learning, and real-time adaptation methods.

---

## N

**Nested Legitimacy** | *Governance Principle*  
Recognition that multiple legitimate authority systems can operate simultaneously without hierarchy, including traditional governance, community councils, bioregional coordination, and constitutional frameworks operating in respectful relationship.

---

## P

**Participatory Budgeting** | *Economic Democracy*  
Community-controlled resource allocation process enabling community members to decide spending priorities for public resources. Integrated with traditional economic systems and Hearts/Leaves currency for democratic economic decision-making.

**Pilot Testing** | *Implementation Method*  
Small-scale experimentation with tools and systems allowing safe testing, community feedback, and adaptation before broader implementation. Includes traditional authority oversight and cultural protocol compliance throughout testing.

**Planetary Health Council (PHC)** | *Institution*  
Meta-governance council setting planetary boundaries and coordinating ecosystem protection under Earth Council moral authority. Includes Indigenous representation, traditional knowledge integration, and community sovereignty protection.

---

## Q

**Quick Start Guide** | *Implementation Resource*  
Simplified pathway for immediate tool use providing essential information and implementation steps for communities beginning GGF implementation. Includes cultural protocol guidance and traditional authority consultation requirements.

---

## R

**Rapid Governance Activation** | *Crisis Tool*  
Emergency protocols for establishing legitimate governance during crisis situations when existing systems are overwhelmed. Integrates traditional emergency leadership with contemporary crisis management maintaining cultural integrity.

**Reflexive Cycle Protocol** | *Learning Method*  
Annual self-assessment process enabling communities and institutions to evaluate effectiveness, adapt approaches, and improve implementation based on community feedback and traditional knowledge guidance.

**Regenerative Economics** | *Economic Philosophy*  
Economic systems serving ecological restoration, community wellbeing, and seven-generation sustainability rather than infinite growth and resource extraction. Based on traditional Indigenous economic principles and Right Relationship.

**Right Relationship** | *Foundational Principle*  
Balanced, regenerative connections between individuals, communities, and the planet based on traditional Indigenous wisdom. Foundation for AUBI economics, traditional governance, and ecological stewardship practices.

---

## S

**Sacred Knowledge** | *Cultural Concept*  
Traditional spiritual, ceremonial, and cultural knowledge requiring special protection from inappropriate sharing, documentation, or commercialization. Protected through traditional protocols and community authority over access and use.

**Seven-Generation Thinking** | *Traditional Wisdom*  
Decision-making principle considering impact on seven generations into the future based on traditional Indigenous governance. Integrated throughout GGF frameworks ensuring long-term sustainability and traditional accountability.

**Social Resilience Council** | *Institution*  
Global governance body overseeing AUBI implementation, Hearts/Leaves distribution, and community economic coordination. Operates under Indigenous guidance with traditional knowledge integration and community sovereignty protection.

**Stack Customization Wizard** | *Technology Tool*  
Backend logic system providing personalized tool recommendations based on Context Assessment Rubric results. Suggests appropriate Implementation Journeys and tool combinations based on community readiness and cultural context.

---

## T

**Tool Maturity Rating** | *Assessment Method*  
Community-generated effectiveness assessment for implementation tools measuring cultural fit, ease of use, resource efficiency, and community impact. Based on community feedback and traditional knowledge evaluation.

**Traditional Authority** | *Governance Concept*  
Legitimate leadership and decision-making power derived from traditional knowledge, cultural protocols, and community recognition rather than external appointment or election. Foundational for Indigenous sovereignty and cultural integrity.

**Traditional Ecological Knowledge (TEK)** | *Knowledge System*  
Indigenous knowledge systems about ecological relationships, land management, and environmental stewardship developed through generations of intimate relationship with specific territories. Protected knowledge requiring community consent for sharing.

**Traditional Knowledge Protection** | *Cultural Protocol*  
Comprehensive safeguards preventing appropriation, commercialization, or inappropriate use of Indigenous knowledge systems. Includes legal protection, community control, benefit-sharing, and cultural authority over knowledge sharing.

**Traditional Knowledge Keeper** | *Cultural Role*  
Community member holding specialized traditional knowledge about cultural practices, ecological relationships, spiritual teachings, or governance systems. Recognized community authority for cultural protocol guidance and traditional wisdom.

**Trauma-Informed Facilitation** | *Method*  
Facilitation approaches recognizing historical and ongoing trauma impacts on community participation and decision-making. Integrates traditional healing practices with contemporary trauma understanding for community safety.

**Treaty for Our Only Home** | *Constitutional Framework*  
Core legal framework providing enforcement mechanisms and institutional reforms for global governance. Establishes democratic legitimacy, Indigenous sovereignty, environmental stewardship, and economic justice as constitutional requirements.

## U

**Universal Access** | *Inclusion Principle*  
Design standards ensuring full participation across disabilities, economic circumstances, cultural differences, and technological access. Includes accessibility accommodation, economic support, cultural translation, and technology alternatives.

## V

**Value Creation Framework** | *Economic Concept*  
System recognizing and rewarding diverse forms of community contribution including care work, cultural preservation, ecological stewardship, and community building rather than only market-based economic activity.

## W

**Watershed Governance** | *Territorial Model*  
Governance organized around complete watershed boundaries from source to mouth, following natural water flow patterns rather than colonial administrative boundaries. Integrates traditional water knowledge with contemporary watershed management.

## Cultural Concepts Requiring Respectful Understanding

### **Concepts from Specific Indigenous Traditions**

**Note**: These concepts are presented with deep respect for their cultural origins. Non-Indigenous readers should understand these as examples of traditional wisdom rather than concepts available for appropriation.

**Buen Vivir** | *Andean Concept*  
South American Indigenous philosophy emphasizing living well in harmony with community and nature rather than individual accumulation or development. Guides alternative economic and governance systems prioritizing wellbeing over growth.

**Haudenosaunee Confederacy** | *Indigenous Governance*  
Traditional governance system of six Indigenous nations demonstrating sophisticated coordination across territories while maintaining individual nation sovereignty. Provides historical model for polycentric governance and traditional diplomacy.

**Kawsay Pacha** | *Quechua Concept*  
Andean Indigenous understanding of living world encompassing all life forms and relationships. Name for Earth Council recognizing Indigenous worldview of interconnected life and traditional ecological wisdom.

**Potlatch** | *Pacific Northwest Indigenous Practice*  
Traditional ceremony and economic system emphasizing gift-giving, resource sharing, and community celebration rather than individual accumulation. Demonstrates traditional abundance economics and community wealth building.

**Talanoa** | *Pacific Island Concept*  
Traditional Pacific dialogue method emphasizing storytelling, relationship-building, and collective understanding before formal decision-making. Provides model for inclusive consultation and traditional consensus building.

**Ubuntu** | *African Philosophy*  
Southern African philosophy emphasizing collective humanity and interdependence expressed as "I am because we are." Guides community-centered governance and economic systems prioritizing collective wellbeing.

## Framework Integration Terms

### **Cross-Framework Connections**

**Framework Interoperability** | *System Design*  
Ability of different GGF frameworks to work together effectively while maintaining their distinct purposes and cultural protocols. Enables coordinated implementation without requiring uniform approaches across diverse communities.

**Constitutional Compliance** | *Legal Requirement*  
Alignment of all GGF tools and frameworks with Treaty for Our Only Home constitutional requirements including democratic participation, Indigenous sovereignty, environmental stewardship, and economic justice.

**Cultural Bridge Building** | *Method*  
Respectful connection and translation between different cultural traditions and governance systems without appropriation or forced assimilation. Enables cross-cultural collaboration while maintaining cultural integrity.

**Nested Systems** | *Organizational Principle*  
Multiple governance systems operating simultaneously at different scales (community, bioregional, constitutional) with clear authority relationships and mutual respect rather than hierarchical control.

## Implementation Context Terms

### **Community Readiness Concepts**

**Governance Maturity** | *Assessment Category*  
Community capacity for collective decision-making, conflict resolution, and democratic participation based on existing governance experience and community organization strength.

**Cultural Coherence** | *Assessment Category*  
Community cultural unity and shared values providing foundation for collective action and traditional knowledge integration in governance implementation.

**Resource Availability** | *Assessment Category*  
Community access to human, financial, and material resources needed for governance tool implementation including traditional knowledge, organizational capacity, and economic resources.

**Conflict Intensity** | *Assessment Category*  
Level of ongoing community conflict affecting collective decision-making capacity and need for healing and reconciliation before effective governance implementation.

## Living Document Protocol

This glossary evolves through ongoing community input and traditional knowledge guidance. Terms are added, modified, or removed based on:

- **Community Feedback**: Implementation experience and community language needs
- **Traditional Knowledge Input**: Elder and traditional knowledge keeper guidance on appropriate terminology
- **Cultural Protocol Compliance**: Ensuring definitions respect traditional knowledge and prevent appropriation
- **Accessibility Requirements**: Clear explanations supporting diverse community understanding
- **Framework Evolution**: New concepts emerging through GGF implementation and community innovation

**Suggest Changes**: Communities and traditional knowledge keepers can suggest glossary improvements through [GGF Community Input Process](../../get-involved/glossary-feedback).

**Cultural Review**: All definitions undergo regular review by Cultural & Ancestral Wisdom Council to ensure cultural appropriateness and traditional knowledge protection.
