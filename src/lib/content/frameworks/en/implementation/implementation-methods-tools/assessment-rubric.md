The Context Assessment Rubric serves as the entry point for communities beginning their implementation journey. Rather than prescribing universal solutions, this diagnostic tool helps communities understand their unique governance context, cultural dynamics, resource capacity, and readiness for different types of interventions. The assessment honors diverse starting points while providing practical guidance for selecting appropriate tools and implementation pathways.

## <a id="indicators"></a>The Four Implementation Readiness Indicators

### **1. Governance Maturity Scale (1-5)**

**Purpose**: Understanding existing governance capacity and decision-making systems

#### **Level 1: Emerging Governance**
**Characteristics**:
- Little formal governance structure or decision-making process
- Decisions made by individuals or very small groups
- Limited community participation in governance
- Informal leadership with unclear accountability

**Strengths**: High flexibility, potential for fresh approaches, less entrenched resistance
**Implementation Considerations**: Start with relationship-building and basic council formation
**Recommended Tools**: Council Convening Playbook, Basic Consensus Tools, Community Mapping

#### **Level 2: Developing Governance**
**Characteristics**:
- Some formal governance structures in place
- Basic decision-making processes established
- Limited but growing community participation
- Leadership roles defined but may lack clear accountability

**Strengths**: Foundation exists to build upon, some community engagement experience
**Implementation Considerations**: Strengthen existing structures while introducing participation
**Recommended Tools**: Institution Chartering Kit, Participatory Planning, Conflict Resolution

#### **Level 3: Functional Governance**
**Characteristics**:
- Clear governance structures with defined roles and processes
- Regular community participation in decision-making
- Established accountability mechanisms
- Effective internal communication systems

**Strengths**: Stable foundation for innovation, established community trust
**Implementation Considerations**: Enhance existing systems with advanced tools and integration
**Recommended Tools**: Citizen Assembly Module, Participatory Budgeting, Digital Integration

#### **Level 4: Sophisticated Governance**
**Characteristics**:
- Complex governance systems with multiple participation pathways
- High community engagement and leadership development
- Strong accountability and feedback mechanisms
- Integration with external networks and systems

**Strengths**: Advanced capacity for innovation and complex coordination
**Implementation Considerations**: Pioneer new approaches and support other communities
**Recommended Tools**: AI Integration, Advanced Consensus, Inter-Community Coordination

#### **Level 5: Exemplary Governance**
**Characteristics**:
- Governance systems that effectively serve community needs
- High levels of participation, accountability, and adaptability
- Strong integration of cultural values and practical effectiveness
- Recognized leadership in governance innovation

**Strengths**: Model for other communities, high innovation capacity
**Implementation Considerations**: Focus on scaling impact and supporting movement ecosystem
**Recommended Tools**: Model Development, Mentorship Systems, Global Coordination

### **2. Conflict Intensity Assessment**

**Purpose**: Understanding conflict dynamics and community healing needs

#### **Peaceful Stability**
**Characteristics**:
- Low levels of interpersonal and intergroup conflict
- Effective informal conflict resolution systems
- High social cohesion and trust levels
- Collaborative decision-making norms

**Implementation Approach**: Focus on maintaining harmony while building capacity
**Conflict-Sensitive Tools**: Consensus-building, collaborative planning, celebration protocols

#### **Manageable Tensions**
**Characteristics**:
- Some ongoing disputes or disagreements
- Occasional heated discussions or relationship strains
- Conflicts generally contained and resolvable
- Community maintains overall functionality

**Implementation Approach**: Strengthen conflict resolution capacity while pursuing development
**Conflict-Sensitive Tools**: Mediation training, restorative circles, communication protocols

#### **Significant Polarization**
**Characteristics**:
- Deep divisions within the community
- Regular heated conflicts or relationship breakdowns
- Difficulty reaching consensus on important decisions
- Some community members feeling excluded or marginalized

**Implementation Approach**: Prioritize healing and relationship repair before major changes
**Conflict-Sensitive Tools**: Truth and reconciliation, healing circles, facilitated dialogue

#### **High Conflict**
**Characteristics**:
- Active interpersonal or intergroup hostility
- Breakdown of communication between community segments
- Governance paralysis due to conflict dynamics
- Community members leaving due to conflict

**Implementation Approach**: Crisis intervention with trauma-informed healing approaches
**Conflict-Sensitive Tools**: Crisis mediation, trauma healing, safety protocols

#### **Crisis or Violence**
**Characteristics**:
- Active violence or threats of violence
- Complete breakdown of governance and communication
- External intervention or law enforcement involvement
- Community facing potential dissolution

**Implementation Approach**: Immediate safety and crisis stabilization before any development work
**Conflict-Sensitive Tools**: Crisis response protocols, safety planning, external mediation

### **3. Resource Availability Matrix**

**Purpose**: Understanding material capacity for implementation

#### **Minimal Resources**
**Characteristics**:
- Volunteer time and community goodwill primarily
- Limited financial resources for governance activities
- Basic meeting spaces and communication methods
- High reliance on contributed time and energy

**Implementation Strategy**: Low-cost, high-impact tools with minimal resource requirements
**Resource-Conscious Tools**: Circle processes, consensus tools, story-based planning

#### **Limited Resources**
**Characteristics**:
- Some dedicated funding for governance activities
- Part-time coordinator or organizer capacity
- Basic technology and communication infrastructure
- Ability to support small events and gatherings

**Implementation Strategy**: Strategic resource use with focus on capacity building
**Resource-Conscious Tools**: Training programs, simple facilitation, basic coordination

#### **Moderate Resources**
**Characteristics**:
- Dedicated staff for governance and community work
- Modest but reliable budget for activities and infrastructure
- Good meeting spaces and communication systems
- Ability to bring in external facilitation and training

**Implementation Strategy**: Comprehensive tool implementation with professional support
**Resource-Conscious Tools**: Citizen assemblies, participatory budgeting, consultant support

#### **Substantial Resources**
**Characteristics**:
- Significant funding for governance innovation and development
- Multiple dedicated staff with specialized skills
- High-quality infrastructure and technology systems
- Capacity for complex projects and external partnership

**Implementation Strategy**: Advanced tool implementation with innovation and leadership development
**Resource-Conscious Tools**: Complex facilitation, technology integration, model development

### **4. Cultural Coherence Check**

**Purpose**: Understanding cultural unity, diversity, and integration needs

#### **Highly Diverse**
**Characteristics**:
- Many different cultural backgrounds and traditions represented
- Multiple languages, religious practices, and worldviews
- Limited shared cultural practices or celebrations
- Potential for both richness and complexity in decision-making

**Cultural Integration Strategy**: Emphasize cultural bridge-building and inclusive practices
**Culturally-Responsive Tools**: Multi-cultural facilitation, translation services, cultural exchange

#### **Mixed Coherence**
**Characteristics**:
- Some shared values and practices with notable differences
- Dominant culture with minority cultural traditions also present
- Some cultural conflicts or misunderstandings
- Growing recognition of need for greater inclusion

**Cultural Integration Strategy**: Build on shared values while honoring differences
**Culturally-Responsive Tools**: Cultural competency training, inclusive decision-making, dialogue

#### **Well Aligned**
**Characteristics**:
- Strong shared values and cultural practices
- Common worldview and approach to community life
- Effective cultural transmission across generations
- Cultural practices integrated into governance

**Cultural Integration Strategy**: Strengthen existing cultural foundation while building governance capacity
**Culturally-Responsive Tools**: Culture-based governance, traditional practices integration

#### **Highly Cohesive**
**Characteristics**:
- Deep cultural unity and shared worldview
- Strong ceremonial and traditional practices
- Effective cultural governance systems
- High resistance to external cultural influence

**Cultural Integration Strategy**: Work within existing cultural framework with minimal external introduction
**Culturally-Responsive Tools**: Traditional governance enhancement, culture-specific tools

## <a id="methodology"></a>Assessment Methodology: Interactive & Culturally Adaptive

### **Multi-Modal Assessment Options**

#### **Individual Self-Assessment (15-20 minutes)**
- **Format**: Online questionnaire with branching logic
- **Benefits**: Quick, private, immediate results
- **Limitations**: Individual perspective only, may miss community dynamics
- **Best For**: Initial exploration, busy community leaders, remote communities

#### **Community Circle Assessment (2-3 hours)**
- **Format**: Facilitated community gathering using traditional circle process
- **Benefits**: Collective wisdom, community buy-in, relationship building
- **Limitations**: Requires facilitation capacity, longer time commitment
- **Best For**: Communities with strong meeting traditions, consensus-oriented cultures

#### **Elder & Knowledge Keeper Consultation (Timeline varies)**
- **Format**: Respectful consultation with traditional leaders and knowledge holders
- **Benefits**: Deep cultural wisdom, traditional governance insights, spiritual guidance
- **Limitations**: May require ceremonial protocols, longer consultation process
- **Best For**: Indigenous communities, tradition-based governance systems

#### **Storytelling Assessment (1-2 days)**
- **Format**: Story-based exploration of community governance history and aspirations
- **Benefits**: Honors oral traditions, reveals historical patterns, emotionally engaging
- **Limitations**: Requires skilled facilitation, longer time commitment
- **Best For**: Oral tradition communities, cultures prioritizing narrative

### **Assessment Question Categories**

#### **Governance Structure Questions**
- "How are important decisions currently made in your community?"
- "Who has formal and informal leadership roles?"
- "What decision-making processes work well? Which don't?"
- "How are community members included in governance?"

#### **Cultural Context Questions**
- "What governance traditions does your community draw from?"
- "How do cultural celebrations and ceremonies relate to community decisions?"
- "What languages are primarily used in community governance?"
- "How are conflicts typically resolved in your cultural tradition?"

#### **Resource Capacity Questions**
- "What time can community members dedicate to governance activities?"
- "What funding is available for governance improvements?"
- "What meeting spaces and communication tools are accessible?"
- "What skills and expertise exist within the community?"

#### **Relationship & Conflict Questions**
- "How would you describe the level of trust in your community?"
- "What are the main sources of disagreement or tension?"
- "How effective are current approaches to resolving conflicts?"
- "Are there community members who feel excluded from decision-making?"

#### **Aspiration & Vision Questions**
- "What does ideal governance look like for your community?"
- "What governance challenges are you most motivated to address?"
- "How quickly does your community prefer to make changes?"
- "What governance innovations are you curious about exploring?"

## <a id="cultural-protocols"></a>Cultural Sensitivity Protocols: Honoring All Ways of Knowing

### **Indigenous Community Protocols**

#### **Free, Prior, and Informed Consent 2.0**
- **Relationship Building**: Establish genuine relationships before any assessment
- **Traditional Timeline**: Honor traditional decision-making timeframes (may be seasonal)
- **Elder Consultation**: Include knowledge keepers and traditional leaders in assessment design
- **Ceremonial Protocols**: Respect ceremonial requirements for community decision-making

#### **Traditional Knowledge Protection**
- **Community Ownership**: Assessment results belong to the community
- **Cultural Confidentiality**: Traditional governance information protected from appropriation
- **Story Sovereignty**: Communities control how their governance stories are shared
- **Benefit Sharing**: Any learning from assessment benefits the contributing community

### **Multi-Cultural Community Protocols**

#### **Language Justice**
- **Multi-Lingual Options**: Assessment available in community languages
- **Cultural Translation**: Concepts adapted for different cultural frameworks
- **Interpretation Support**: Professional interpretation for community discussions
- **Visual & Oral Options**: Non-text-based assessment methods available

#### **Religious & Spiritual Integration**
- **Sacred Space Recognition**: Assessment can include ceremonial or prayer elements
- **Spiritual Governance**: Traditional spiritual leadership roles acknowledged
- **Ritual Integration**: Assessment adapted for communities with ritual decision-making
- **Multi-Faith Inclusion**: Recognition of diverse spiritual traditions in governance

### **Trauma-Informed Assessment**

#### **Historical Trauma Awareness**
- **Colonial Impact Recognition**: Understanding how colonization affects governance capacity
- **Healing-Centered Approach**: Assessment supports rather than retraumatizes communities
- **Strengths Focus**: Emphasis on community resilience and existing wisdom
- **Safety Protocols**: Emotional safety prioritized throughout assessment process

#### **Current Trauma Considerations**
- **Crisis Sensitivity**: Assessment adapted for communities experiencing current trauma
- **Trigger Awareness**: Questions designed to avoid retraumatizing community members
- **Support Integration**: Connection to healing resources and trauma-informed tools
- **Flexibility**: Assessment can be paused, modified, or rescheduled based on community needs

## <a id="scoring"></a>Scoring System & Tool Recommendations

### **Implementation Readiness Score Calculation**

#### **Weighted Scoring System**
- **Governance Maturity (30%)**: Foundation capacity for implementing tools
- **Cultural Coherence (25%)**: Ability to adapt tools to community context
- **Resource Availability (25%)**: Material capacity for tool implementation
- **Conflict Intensity (20%)**: Need for healing-focused vs. development-focused approaches

#### **Cultural Adjustment Factors**
- **Traditional Governance Bonus**: +10 points for communities with strong traditional governance
- **Innovation History Bonus**: +5 points for communities with successful innovation experience
- **External Partnership Bonus**: +5 points for communities with effective external partnerships
- **Crisis Experience Bonus**: +10 points for communities that have successfully navigated crisis

## <a id="wizard"></a>Stack Customization Wizard: AI-Assisted Tool Selection

### **Human-AI Collaboration Model**

#### **AI Capabilities**
- **Pattern Recognition**: Identifying communities with similar profiles and successful tool combinations
- **Resource Optimization**: Matching tool requirements to available community resources
- **Cultural Flagging**: Identifying potential cultural sensitivity issues for human review
- **Integration Mapping**: Suggesting tools that work well together

#### **Human Oversight**
- **Cultural Review**: All AI recommendations reviewed by human facilitators with cultural competency
- **Community Final Decision**: AI provides suggestions; communities make final selections
- **Cultural Adaptation**: Human facilitators help adapt AI suggestions to cultural context
- **Relationship Building**: Human connection maintained throughout recommendation process

### **Wizard Process Flow**

#### **Step 1: Context Integration**
- Assessment results analyzed alongside community-provided context
- Cultural background and governance traditions identified
- Resource constraints and opportunities mapped
- Timeline and urgency preferences incorporated

#### **Step 2: Pattern Matching**
- Similar community profiles identified from global database
- Successful tool combinations from similar contexts highlighted
- Failure patterns from similar contexts flagged for avoidance
- Innovation opportunities based on unique community characteristics

#### **Step 3: Tool Stack Generation**
- Primary pathway tools selected based on journey recommendation
- Complementary tools added based on specific community characteristics
- Resource requirements calculated and matched to community capacity
- Implementation timeline estimated based on community preferences

#### **Step 4: Cultural Adaptation Guidance**
- Cultural sensitivity flags identified for community consideration
- Traditional governance integration opportunities highlighted
- Language justice and accessibility requirements noted
- Ceremonial and spiritual integration options suggested

#### **Step 5: Community Review & Customization**
- Generated stack presented to community for review and modification
- Community feedback incorporated into final tool selection
- Implementation sequence adjusted based on community priorities
- Support resources and training identified based on selected tools

## <a id="adaptation"></a>Adaptation & Continuous Learning: Assessment Evolution

### **Community Feedback Integration**

#### **Post-Implementation Assessment**
- **3-Month Check-in**: How well did recommended tools match community needs?
- **1-Year Evaluation**: What adaptations were necessary? What worked well?
- **Success Pattern Documentation**: Capturing effective community adaptations
- **Failure Analysis**: Understanding when recommendations didn't work and why

#### **Algorithm Improvement**
- **Recommendation Accuracy Tracking**: Measuring how often AI suggestions prove effective
- **Cultural Sensitivity Monitoring**: Identifying and correcting cultural bias in recommendations
- **Pattern Recognition Enhancement**: Improving AI ability to recognize successful community types
- **Integration Effectiveness**: Understanding which tool combinations work well together

### **Assessment Methodology Evolution**

#### **Cultural Expansion**
- **New Cultural Protocols**: Developing assessment methods for newly encountered governance traditions
- **Language Addition**: Expanding assessment availability in additional languages
- **Traditional Knowledge Integration**: Incorporating traditional diagnostic methods from Indigenous communities
- **Spiritual Assessment Methods**: Developing assessment approaches for spiritually-oriented communities

#### **Question Refinement**
- **Community Testing**: New assessment questions tested with diverse communities
- **Cultural Translation**: Ensuring questions work across different cultural contexts
- **Bias Elimination**: Identifying and removing cultural, economic, or educational bias
- **Accessibility Improvement**: Making assessment more accessible to diverse ability and literacy levels

### **Global Learning Network**

#### **Cross-Community Learning**
- **Assessment Sharing**: Communities can share their assessment results with global network (with permission)
- **Pattern Recognition**: Global database enables identification of successful governance patterns
- **Innovation Diffusion**: Successful tool adaptations shared across similar communities
- **Challenge Support**: Communities facing similar challenges connected for mutual support

#### **Research Integration**
- **Academic Partnership**: Collaboration with governance and anthropology researchers
- **Traditional Knowledge Documentation**: Respectful documentation of traditional governance assessment methods
- **Effectiveness Research**: Long-term studies of assessment accuracy and community outcomes
- **Innovation Tracking**: Monitoring emergence of new governance innovations from assessed communities
