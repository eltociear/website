# Bioregional Polis At-a-Glance
*An Indigenous-Guided Urban Framework*

## The Vision in One Sentence
Transform cities into regenerative ecosystems guided by Indigenous wisdom, where citizens govern through bioregional democracy within Indigenous-led territories.

## What It Is
A comprehensive framework reimagining urban governance as living covenants between human and non-human communities, rooted in Indigenous stewardship and powered by regenerative economics.

## The Challenge We Address
- Cities consume 78% of global energy and produce 70% of carbon emissions
- Current urban systems disconnect communities from bioregional foundations
- Urban governance often excludes Indigenous voices despite territorial sovereignty
- Environmental degradation accelerates while social inequity deepens

## Our Core Innovation: The Indigenous-Led Citizen Assembly
**40% Indigenous representatives + 30% community members + 20% youth + 10% urban ecologists**

Operating through ceremonial governance with FPIC 2.0 protocols and veto power over decisions affecting traditional territories.

## How It Works

### **Foundation**: Indigenous Sovereignty
All urban governance operates within Indigenous-led Bioregional Autonomous Zones (BAZs) under Earth Council moral authority

### **Economics**: Regenerative Incentives
Hearts/Leaves currencies logged in Love Ledger reward:
- Civic participation in assemblies
- Urban gardening and stewardship
- Cultural festivals and ceremonies
- Biophilic business practices

### **Technology**: Ethical & Inclusive
- AI governance under Aurora Accord
- Indigenous data sovereignty protocols
- Low-tech alternatives for digital equity
- Community-controlled monitoring systems

### **Justice**: Rights for All Beings
- Legal personhood for urban ecosystems
- Disability Impact Assessments for all policies
- Gender/queer spatial justice audits
- Youth veto power on long-term decisions

## Entry Points for Different Actors

### **Cities & Municipalities**
- **Observer**: Access tools and peer learning
- **Pilot**: Implement selected components with support
- **Partner**: Full adoption with funding integration
- **Leader**: Pioneer new approaches and best practices

### **Indigenous Communities**
- Document territorial relationships to urban areas
- Establish FPIC 2.0 and Red Lines procedures
- Develop data sovereignty protocols
- Build alliances with urban partners

### **Citizens & Organizations**
- Learn bioregional and Indigenous history
- Participate in existing assemblies
- Advocate for ecosystem rights
- Start local regenerative projects

### **Funders & Investors**
- Direct capital toward regenerative urban projects
- Partner with Global Security & Exploration Trust
- Support community-controlled development
- Fund Indigenous-led research and innovation
