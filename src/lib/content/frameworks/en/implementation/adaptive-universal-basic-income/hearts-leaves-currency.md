# Hearts & Leaves Currency: The Circulation of Care

> *"What if money could remember kindness? What if every transaction strengthened the web of relationships that sustains life? Hearts and Leaves currencies transform exchange from extraction to circulation, creating economic flows that heal rather than harm our communities and ecosystems."*

AUBI operates through innovative dual-track currency systems that make visible and reward the work that traditional economics ignores, fully integrated with the Financial Systems Framework's infrastructure while maintaining community sovereignty and cultural adaptation. These currencies embody the principle that money is a social technology that can either separate communities through scarcity or connect them through abundance.

## The Philosophy of Care Currencies

Traditional money functions as a store of value that individuals accumulate for personal security, creating incentives for hoarding rather than circulation. Hearts and Leaves currencies operate on different principles inspired by Indigenous gift economies where wealth strengthens through sharing rather than accumulation.

**Money as Relationship**: Rather than treating money as neutral commodity, Hearts and Leaves currencies encode values and relationships directly into their design. Every Hearts transaction acknowledges care relationships. Every Leaves token represents healing relationship with natural systems. The currencies themselves become tools for building rather than commodifying community connections.

Dr. Williams experiences this when Hearts payments for community health education flow through chartered Community Providers rather than accumulating in personal accounts. The currency maintains value through institutional circulation that strengthens care relationships rather than concentration that separates caregivers from communities.

**Circulation Over Accumulation**: Hearts and Leaves currencies include design features that encourage circulation rather than hoarding. Hearts maintain purchasing power when they flow through care economies via Community Providers. Leaves tokens generate maximum ecological benefit when they fund restoration projects rather than remaining in digital wallets.

Maya's community demonstrates circulation principles when Hearts earned by Community Providers for traditional knowledge teaching flow to support elder care services, which generate Hearts for youth caregivers, which fund cultural education programs that complete the cycle by supporting traditional knowledge preservation.

**Regenerative Value Creation**: Unlike extractive currencies that gain value by depleting resources or exploiting labor, Hearts and Leaves currencies create value through healing—healing relationships, healing communities, healing ecosystems. The currencies grow stronger as communities become more resilient rather than more dependent.

## The Dual-Track System: Monetized and Non-Monetized Care

AUBI operates through two parallel mechanisms that serve different aspects of the care economy while maintaining cultural integrity and preventing commodification of sacred relationships:

### Track 1: Monetized Hearts Economy

**Community Provider Model**: Hearts are earned exclusively by **chartered Community Providers**—formal organizations, cooperatives, and institutions that provide care services within communities. These providers undergo certification processes that ensure alignment with Right Relationship principles and community benefit.

Citizens receive care services from Community Providers and pay using Hearts from their Layer 1 baseline or by purchasing Hearts with traditional currency. Providers then redeem accumulated Hearts at the **Hearts Treasury** for fiat currency, creating a stable economic circulation that supports professional care work.

Dr. Williams works with a chartered Community Health Provider that earns Hearts for comprehensive health education, traditional medicine integration, and preventive care services. Community members use their Hearts allocations to access these services, while the provider redeems Hearts for operational funding and staff compensation.

**Proof of Care Protocol**: Community Providers demonstrate care impact through formal **Proof of Care** verification that documents community benefit, cultural appropriateness, and Right Relationship alignment. This institutional verification prevents gaming while ensuring Hearts circulation serves authentic community needs.

### Track 2: Non-Monetized Love Ledger

**Gratitude Token System**: The Love Ledger operates independently of Hearts currency, using non-monetized **Gratitude Tokens** to recognize informal care, kinship relationships, and community contributions that should never be commercialized.

Maya's grandmother teaching traditional plant knowledge to children receives Gratitude Tokens that acknowledge her wisdom transmission without transforming sacred teaching into economic transaction. These tokens build social recognition and community cohesion while informing LMCI measurements.

**LMCI Data Source**: Love Ledger Gratitude Tokens provide qualitative data for Love, Meaning, and Connection Index calculations, capturing relational wealth and community vitality that exists outside monetary systems while contributing to overall community wellbeing assessment.

**Cultural Protection**: The non-monetized nature of Love Ledger protects sacred knowledge, traditional practices, and kinship care from commercialization while enabling recognition and community validation of essential contributions that sustain cultural and spiritual life.

## Hearts Currency: Professional Care Economy Infrastructure

**Hearts Currency** serves as the primary medium of exchange for professional care economy activities, operating through chartered Community Providers while maintaining community sovereignty and cultural adaptation.

### Community Provider Certification and Operation

**Certification Process**: Organizations seeking Community Provider status undergo assessment for community benefit, cultural sensitivity, Right Relationship alignment, and operational capacity. Certification involves community consultation, Indigenous leadership approval, and ongoing performance monitoring.

Certification criteria include democratic governance structures, community accountability mechanisms, cultural protocol respect, environmental sustainability, and commitment to serving community members regardless of ability to pay Hearts.

**Provider Types**: Community Providers include health cooperatives, educational collectives, elder care organizations, cultural preservation societies, conflict resolution centers, and environmental restoration groups that demonstrate authentic community service and Right Relationship principles.

Ambassador Chen's island nation includes Community Providers for traditional navigation education, coastal restoration, cultural ceremony facilitation, and climate adaptation planning that earn Hearts while strengthening traditional practices and community resilience.

**Performance Monitoring**: Community Providers undergo regular community evaluation, LMCI impact assessment, and cultural appropriateness review to maintain certification. Community members provide feedback through traditional governance processes and Love Ledger Gratitude Tokens.

### Hearts Treasury: Stability and Redemption Infrastructure

**Financial Clearinghouse Function**: The Hearts Treasury operates as a financial clearinghouse, managed in partnership with local credit unions and community banks, that guarantees fiat redemption for Hearts at stable exchange rates.

The Treasury maintains reserves through Global Commons Fund capitalization and ongoing levies from automation taxes, resource extraction fees, and digital commons dividends that ensure Hearts maintain purchasing power and redemption reliability.

**Stability Mechanisms**: Hearts value remains stable through asset backing including renewable energy infrastructure, ecosystem restoration bonds, and community-controlled enterprises that generate ongoing value through regenerative practices rather than extractive operations.

Maya's community Treasury maintains stability through coral restoration bond backing, traditional fishing cooperative ownership, and renewable energy system investments that create value through ecosystem healing and community economic sovereignty.

**Redemption Protocols**: Community Providers redeem Hearts through standardized protocols that include impact verification, community feedback integration, and ongoing certification compliance. Redemption rates reflect both economic stability and community benefit assessment.

**Local Partnership Integration**: Hearts Treasury operates through partnerships with credit unions, community banks, and cooperative financial institutions that share commitment to community economic sovereignty and democratic financial governance rather than extractive profit maximization.

### Hearts Circulation and Business Integration

**Community Provider Networks**: Hearts circulate through networks of certified Community Providers that offer complementary services including health, education, cultural preservation, environmental restoration, and community organizing that create comprehensive care ecosystems.

Citizens use Hearts allocations to access services across provider networks, creating circulation patterns that strengthen community relationships while ensuring care workers receive fair compensation for professional services.

**Progressive Business Integration**: Traditional businesses can achieve limited Hearts integration through community benefit demonstration, cooperative governance adoption, and commitment to Right Relationship principles that serve community flourishing alongside financial sustainability.

Dr. Williams purchases locally grown food, traditional medicine supplies, and cooperative childcare services using Hearts earned through her Community Health Provider, creating economic flows that strengthen community health infrastructure while meeting family needs.

**Tax Incentives and Policy Support**: Governments provide tax advantages and regulatory benefits for businesses that accept Hearts currency and adopt cooperative governance structures that serve community wellbeing alongside financial sustainability, creating positive feedback loops for Hearts adoption.

## Leaves Currency: Ecological Restoration Recognition

**Leaves Currency** operates as specialized tokens rewarding specific ecological restoration activities that contribute to planetary healing while building community resilience and economic sovereignty under community and Indigenous control.

### Ecological Contribution Verification

**Community-Controlled Restoration**: Leaves reward verified ecological restoration activities led by Indigenous communities and local organizations that combine traditional knowledge with contemporary restoration science while maintaining community ownership of restoration outcomes.

Ambassador Chen's island nation earns Leaves for mangrove restoration that combines traditional planting protocols with contemporary marine science, providing coastal protection, fisheries habitat, and carbon sequestration while maintaining community sovereignty over marine resources and traditional territory.

**Traditional Knowledge Integration**: Leaves recognition prioritizes restoration activities that integrate Indigenous traditional ecological knowledge with contemporary restoration practices, ensuring that ecological work strengthens rather than appropriates traditional knowledge while contributing to global climate resilience.

**Scientific Validation Standards**: Ecological impact receives verification through community-controlled monitoring systems that combine traditional knowledge indicators with contemporary scientific measurement, ensuring restoration effectiveness while maintaining community authority over monitoring protocols.

### Green Job Score Enhancement

**Fractal Labor Parliament Standards**: The Green Job Score from the Work in Liberation Framework directly multiplies Leaves rewards based on ecological impact and community benefit, creating incentives for restoration activities that serve both environmental and social justice.

**Ecological Impact Assessment**: Activities receive scoring from 1.0x to 1.5x based on measurable environmental benefits including carbon sequestration rates, biodiversity improvement, water quality enhancement, and soil health restoration, with community-controlled monitoring ensuring accurate assessment.

Maya's coral restoration project receives 1.4x Leaves multiplier because it combines high ecological impact with Indigenous leadership, traditional knowledge integration, and community ownership of restoration outcomes that serve both local resilience and global marine ecosystem health.

**Community Benefit Evaluation**: Scoring includes social justice factors such as Indigenous leadership, community ownership, local employment creation, and traditional knowledge integration, with projects led by frontline communities receiving higher multipliers than corporate-controlled initiatives.

**Long-term Restoration Commitment**: Leaves recognition includes ongoing stewardship requirements that ensure restoration activities receive long-term maintenance and community care rather than short-term project completion that might not achieve lasting ecological benefit.

### Carbon Market and Environmental Integration

**Community-Priority Carbon Credits**: Leaves integrate with existing carbon markets while prioritizing community-controlled projects that receive preference pricing and ensure revenue flows support ongoing restoration rather than external profit extraction.

Carbon credit integration includes requirements that traditional knowledge holders receive recognition and benefit-sharing from carbon market participation while maintaining community control over traditional territory and restoration decision-making.

**Treaty Ecocide Enforcement Integration**: Leaves token systems connect to Treaty for Our Only Home ecocide enforcement mechanisms, providing positive incentives for ecological restoration while supporting legal consequences for environmental destruction.

**Environmental Justice Priority**: Leaves distribution prioritizes frontline communities most affected by pollution and climate change, ensuring that ecological restoration serves environmental justice rather than merely offsetting wealthy consumer emissions through distant restoration projects.
