### ‚ùì **Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: How does this respect national sovereignty?**
**A:** Framework operates within existing UNCLOS law, providing coordination support rather than governance replacement. Nations maintain full control over territorial waters while gaining access to funding, technology, and cooperation benefits.

**Q: Is the $300M budget feasible?**
**A:** Yes‚Äîrepresents just 1.4% of current $22B in harmful fishing subsidies. Economic modeling shows 6:1 returns through ecosystem services, with Costa Rica demonstrating economic growth from eliminating harmful subsidies.

**Q: How do you prevent corporate capture?**
**A:** Indigenous veto power, limited corporate representation (10% voting), transparent blockchain funding, community oversight, and diverse funding sources prevent any single entity from controlling the framework.

**Q: What about communities lacking technology?**
**A:** Framework includes low-tech participation pathways (radio reporting, paper monitoring, community coordinators) with equal weighting of all contributions regardless of technology access.
