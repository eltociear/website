Let's start with the mechanism that makes everything work:

**Currently:** Your survival depends on selling your labor.
- Lose your job → lose healthcare
- Can't pay rent → homelessness
- No savings → desperation
- Desperation → accept any terms

**Result:** Employers have structural leverage. They can

**The oligarchy doesn't need whips and chains. Conditional survival is the control mechanism.**

- Baseline income covers housing, food, healthcare, basics
- Lose a bad job → inconvenience, not catastrophe
- Savings possible → time to find better
- Options available → negotiate from strength

**Result:** Labor gets ultimate bargaining power. Employers must offer genuine value—meaningful work, fair pay, decent conditions—or nobody shows up.

**This isn't about being lazy. It's about having options.**

And when labor has options, capital has to compete for it. The whole power dynamic inverts.

## Why "Tax the Rich" Keeps Failing: Stock vs. Flow

You might be thinking: "This sounds great, but the UK is broke. We can't afford the NHS, let alone a Sovereign Floor."

**This is the scarcity trap.**

The UK isn't broke. The City of London processes trillions in derivatives every day. The land under our feet—often owned by the same families for centuries—has appreciated by billions without the owners lifting a finger. Britain's financial sector moves more money in a week than the entire economy produces in a year.

**The money is there. It's just stuck in the ceiling (Assets/Speculation) instead of circulating on the floor (People/Trade).**

Here's how we install the plumbing to bring it down.

Before we get to AUBI's funding mechanisms, you need to understand why traditional wealth taxes fail.

**The problem:** Wealth is mobile. Capital can hide.

- Try to tax billionaires → they move to Monaco or Switzerland
- Try to tax corporations → they shift profits to Ireland or Luxembourg  
- Try to tax stock holdings → they take loans against assets (never "sell," never taxed)
- Try to close loopholes → they hire better lawyers

**"Tax the rich" sounds good. But if the rich can dodge, it's theater.**

**The reframe:** Stop trying to tax the stock (wealth that can hide). Tax the flows and enclosures (wealth that cannot hide).

### What You Can't Move to the Cayman Islands

**1. Land**

You literally cannot offshore real estate. It sits there.

**Land Value Tax (LVT):** Tax the value of land, not improvements on it.

- Aristocrat owns Mayfair property → pay tax on location value
- Speculative vacant lots → pay annual carrying cost
- Productive farms → taxed on land value, not farmer's hard work

**Why this works:**
- Discourages speculation (expensive to hold land doing nothing)
- Encourages development (building doesn't increase tax)
- Captures socially-created value (location value created by community infrastructure, not owner)
- Can't be offshored (land doesn't move)

**The UK connection:** This isn't radical—it's old British radical thought. **Winston Churchill** advocated for LVT in 1909. **Henry George**'s *Progress and Poverty* (1879) influenced the UK's early progressive movement. The idea that landowners shouldn't capture value created by railways, schools, and public investment was mainstream Labour policy once.

**Council Tax vs. LVT:** Council Tax taxes property value (penalizes improvements). LVT taxes land value (penalizes holding valuable land unproductively). If you build, improve, or use land well under LVT, your tax doesn't go up. Under Council Tax, it does.

**Precedent:** Singapore funds 30% of government from LVT. Estonia uses it successfully. Denmark has partial LVT. Even Hong Kong's economy is partially funded from land value capture.

**UK-specific target:** London land owned by aristocratic families since medieval times. That value wasn't created by them—it was created by centuries of public investment (Tube, utilities, culture, commerce). LVT captures it.

**2. Carbon/Resource Extraction**

You cannot drill oil in Texas and claim it happened in Bermuda.

**Carbon Dividends:** Tax carbon emissions, return revenue as dividends.

- Fossil fuel companies pay based on emissions
- Citizens receive equal share of revenue
- Revenue neutral (no net tax increase)
- Creates constituency for carbon pricing (everyone benefits)

**Precedent:** British Columbia's carbon tax ($30/ton, revenue neutral). Alaska Permanent Fund (oil revenue → dividends to residents).

**Resource Taxes:** Extraction fees on minerals, timber, water, soil nutrients.

- Mining companies pay for mineral extraction
- Logging companies pay for forest use
- Agriculture pays for soil depletion (regenerative practices get rebates)

**Why this works:** Resources belong to commons, extraction is privilege not right, revenue funds AUBI baseline.

**3. Financial Transactions (The Algorithmic Toll)**

High-frequency trading (HFT) moves trillions daily through The City—London's financial district. A tiny tax on each transaction captures enormous revenue.

**Financial Transaction Tax (FTT):** 0.1% on stock/bond/derivative trades.

- HFT firm trading £1 billion daily pays £1 million annually
- Long-term investor barely affected (few transactions)
- Discourages speculation, encourages productive investment

**Why this works:**
- Massive tax base (global financial transactions: $5+ quadrillion annually, massive chunk through London)
- Can't avoid (transaction happens or it doesn't)
- Targets extraction (speculation), not production (investment)
- Technically feasible (electronic transactions are tracked)

**The UK advantage:** London is the global hub for derivatives trading. The City processes more financial transactions than New York. **Britain is uniquely positioned to capture this revenue** because so much fictitious capital flows through UK infrastructure.

**Precedent:** 
- UK already has **Stamp Duty Reserve Tax** (0.5% on UK shares)—generates £3-4 billion annually
- France and Italy use FTT successfully (lower rates, better implementation)
- Sweden tried it (1984-1991), poorly designed, abandoned—but we've learned from those mistakes

**The upgrade:** Apply specifically to algorithmic/HFT, not all trades. Tax the casino, not the investors. Target the parasitic layer (nanosecond arbitrage) not the productive layer (long-term capital allocation).

**Revenue estimate (UK):** £15-25 billion annually at 0.1% rate on relevant transactions.

**4. Automation Productivity Gains**

When a company replaces 100 workers with AI/robots and productivity soars, that's socially-created value (built on collective knowledge, public infrastructure, educated workforce).

**Automation Tax:** 5-25% on productivity gains from automation.

- Company automates warehouse, saves $10M in labor costs → pays tax on portion of savings
- Graduated rate: >30% productivity gain or >10% labor displacement triggers tax
- Revenue funds AUBI, especially for displaced workers

**Why this works:**
- Captures value created by collective investment (education, infrastructure, research)
- Doesn't punish automation (companies still profit)
- Funds transition for workers displaced by technology
- Aligns incentives (automation benefits everyone, not just shareholders)

**Precedent:** Bill Gates proposed this. South Korea exploring it. No full implementation yet—this is frontier territory.

**5. Digital Commons Dividends**

Data, attention, platform network effects—all socially created value that corporations currently capture privately.

**Mechanisms:**
- Data dividend (your data generates value → you get paid)
- Platform tax (network effects are commons → platform pays for access)
- Algorithmic value capture (recommendation systems trained on collective behavior → revenue share)

**Why this works:** Makes explicit that digital commons are commons, not corporate property.

**Precedent:** EU's GDPR (data rights). California's CCPA. No revenue models yet, but rights infrastructure exists.

## The AUBI Architecture: Four Layers

Now that you understand funding mechanisms, here's what AUBI actually delivers:

### Layer 0: The Invisible Foundation (Relational Wealth)

This isn't a payment. It's recognition.

**What it honors:** Kinship, cultural wisdom, ecological care, community care—all the things capitalism treats as worthless but human flourishing requires.

**How it works:** Recorded in Love Ledger (non-monetized gratitude token system), celebrated in community gatherings, no cash payout but social recognition.

**Why it matters:** Restores dignity to "unproductive" activities. Your grandmother's care work was always valuable—now it's seen.

**Translation for Dickie:** Think of this as the relationship reputation system in a game—doesn't give you gold, but affects how NPCs interact with you and what quests become available.

### Layer 1: Universal Baseline (The Strike Fund)

**What you get:** Enough to cover basic needs. Not luxury, but genuine security.

**Amount:** Calculated regionally to cover:
- Housing: 40% (rent/mortgage + utilities)
- Food: 25%
- Healthcare: 10% (beyond NHS)
- Transport: 10%
- Other basics: 15%

**In the UK context:** Roughly £1,200-1,800/month depending on region (London higher, rural areas lower).

**Payment format:** This is where we need a dual-currency mechanism.

**The problem with single-currency AUBI:** If you pay everything in pounds, two things happen:
1. The wealthy can hoard it (defeating the purpose)
2. It doesn't distinguish between market economy needs and care economy needs

**The solution: Complementary currencies.**

70% paid in standard fiat (pounds—covers rent, groceries from Tesco, utility bills).

30% paid in a separate care economy currency that:
- Can only be spent on care services (childcare, eldercare, mental health support, community services)
- Cannot be hoarded or speculated with
- Builds a parallel economy less vulnerable to extraction

**In the Global Governance Frameworks architecture, we code these currencies as "Hearts" (care economy) and later introduce "Leaves" (ecological work).** But the concept is straightforward: we need money for different purposes that can't all be captured by pounds.

**Why hybrid payment:**
- Pounds cover market economy needs (rent, food from corporate chains)
- Care currency covers care economy needs (services from community providers)
- Builds parallel infrastructure that's extraction-resistant

**Eligibility:** Everyone 18+. No means testing, no work requirements, no paperwork proving you deserve to exist.

**Youth option:** Ages 16-18 can opt into civic internships for early AUBI + skill development.

**The political economy:** This is permanent, unconditional, universal. You cannot lose it. It's yours by virtue of being human.

**This is what breaks the exploitation model.** You can say no to bad work because survival isn't conditional.

### Layer 2: Contribution Bonus (Dignity Restoration)

**What you get:** Additional care economy currency (Hearts) for contributing to things capitalism undervalues.

**Contributions that count:**
- **Love:** Caregiving (children, elderly, disabled), mentorship, conflict resolution
- **Meaning:** Teaching, cultural preservation, spiritual care, art
- **Connection:** Ecological restoration, community organizing, mutual aid

**How it works:** 
- Only chartered Community Providers (verified organizations—think registered charities, community groups, cooperatives) can earn Layer 2
- You spend your care currency (Hearts) at these providers for services
- Providers accumulate Hearts from multiple users
- Providers redeem accumulated Hearts at a Hearts Treasury (municipal/regional clearinghouse) for pounds
- Verification through Proof of Care protocol (audit trail prevents gaming)

**Think of it like this:** 
- Market economy: You work → get paid pounds → spend at Tesco
- Care economy: You contribute → get paid Hearts → spend at community childcare → they redeem Hearts for pounds at Treasury

**Amount:** Up to 50% of baseline (capped higher for low-income, lower for wealthy—progressive).

**UK example:** Someone on £1,500/month baseline could earn up to £750/month additional in Hearts through verified care contributions.

**Why this matters:** This explicitly rewards the work that "doesn't count" under capitalism. Caregiving, community building, cultural preservation—now they're economically valued, not just morally praised.

**The anti-shame mechanism:** You're not a "welfare recipient." You're a care economy participant. Your contributions matter and are compensated.

### Layer 3: Equity Bonus (Reparative Justice)

**What you get:** Additional Hearts addressing historical/ongoing injustices.

**Coverage:**
- Health/disability support (extra for medical needs, accessibility, care requirements)
- Geographic equity (rural/remote areas get adjustment for higher costs)
- **Reparations:** Direct payments for descendants of enslaved peoples, colonized communities, Indigenous peoples
- Refugee/migrant support (portable LMCI scores cross borders)

**Amount:** Variable based on need/harm assessment. Substantial—not token gestures.

**Why this matters:** Acknowledges that "equality" on an unequal playing field isn't justice. This actively repairs historical damage.

**The negative adjustment:** Conviction for ecocide or severe human rights violations can reduce AUBI by up to 50%. (Wealthy people who harm the planet pay a price.)

### Leaves: The Ecological Recognition System

**A separate currency for a separate purpose.**

Just as Hearts prevents care work from being hoarded or speculated with, we need a currency for ecological work that can't be financialized.

**Leaves** are non-fungible tokens (think: digital badges that can't be sold) earned for verified ecological work:
- Rewilding Scottish Highlands
- Peat bog restoration
- Tree planting schemes
- Renewable energy installation
- Permaculture farming
- Urban biodiversity projects

**Green Job Score:** Multiplier (1.0x-1.5x) based on ecological impact, validated by labor standards bodies (in GGF: Fractal Labor Parliament—but in UK context: think verified by Environment Agency / Natural England equivalent).

**Why separate from Hearts:** Keeps ecological value distinct from market value. You can't sell Leaves for cash, but they grant:
- Community status and recognition
- Priority access to future ecological projects
- Proof of contribution for green sector opportunities
- Potential tie-ins with council housing priority, local services access

**UK context:** Imagine if restoring Yorkshire moors or cleaning Thames tributaries earned you Leaves that gave you:
- Housing queue priority in eco-villages
- Free access to National Trust properties
- Recognition in community assemblies

**Logged separately, not monetized.** This is recognition and opportunity, not commodification.

## The Failure Modes

### Failure Mode 1: Inflation

**The claim:** "If you give everyone money, prices just go up!"

**The reality:** Only if supply is constrained.

**The mechanisms:**
- AUBI includes housing support → incentive to build more housing
- Increased demand for goods → increased production (more jobs)
- Basic needs are elastic (can increase supply)
- Luxury goods might inflate, but that's fine (not survival goods)

**The evidence:** Alaska Permanent Fund didn't cause inflation. Stockton pilot didn't cause inflation. GiveDirectly (Kenya) caused minor, temporary inflation that stabilized.

**The safeguard:** AUBI adjusts to regional price indices. If housing costs spike, baseline adjusts up, funded by Land Value Tax (which captures speculative gains).

### Failure Mode 2: "People Will Stop Working"

**The claim:** "Why work if survival is free?"

**The reality:** Humans want more than survival. We want purpose, status, community, accomplishment.

**The evidence:**
- Alaska dividend didn't reduce employment
- Finland UBI pilot: no employment decline
- Stockton pilot: full-time work increased
- Psychological research: autonomy increases motivation

**The mechanism:** AUBI removes desperation work (three exploitative jobs just to survive), replaces with meaningful work (one job you choose because it matters).

**The both/and:** Some people will work less (new parents, students, caregivers, artists). That's a feature, not a bug. That's care work being valued.

### Failure Mode 3: Migration Pressure

**The claim:** "Everyone will move to cities with AUBI!"

**The reality:** Gradual rollout + portability prevents this.

**The mechanisms:**
- Start with pilots in few cities
- Scale over 5-10 years
- Eventually universal (federal/global)
- Make AUBI portable (you take it with you)

**The evidence:** EU's freedom of movement didn't collapse social systems. People don't just move for benefits—they move for jobs, family, community.

**The safeguard:** Phase in slowly. By the time it's universal, there's no arbitrage opportunity.

### Failure Mode 4: Capital Flight

**The claim:** "Rich people and companies will leave!"

**The reality:** Capital needs access to markets and infrastructure. That's not mobile.

**The mechanisms:**
- You can't offshore land (LVT stays)
- You can't offshore transactions (FTT stays)
- You can't offshore resource extraction (taxes stay)
- Companies need customers (AUBI creates purchasing power)

**The threat is overblown.** Some rich individuals will leave. Fine. The system still works because wealth taxes are only ~10% of funding. The rest comes from flows they can't dodge.

**The precedent:** EU's high-tax countries (Denmark, Sweden) didn't hemorrhage capital. People and businesses stay because infrastructure, education, social stability are valuable.
