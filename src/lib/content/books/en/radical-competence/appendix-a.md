**Competition or Allies?**

Some groups might seem like competition (also working on housing, also organizing community). 

**The test:**
- Do they share your values (community ownership, not extraction)?
- Are they willing to collaborate (not territorial)?
- Do they have different strengths (you coordinate, they have networks)?

**If yes to all three:** Allies, not competition.
**If no to any:** Keep friendly distance, don't burn bridges, but don't depend on them.

## Can't Find the Data? (Fallback Strategies)

**Don't let missing data stop you. Here's what to do:**

**Budget numbers unknown?**
- Use last year's headline figure from local news
- Or: Email council finance department directly ("FOI request: total council budget")
- Direction matters more than precision

**No extraction estimates?**
- Use the **Housing Quick Math** from Section 3
- It's usually 60-80% of total extraction anyway
- Conservative estimate beats no estimate

**Don't know who the allies are?**
- Search "[Your city] community land trust"
- Search "[Your city] cooperative network"
- Search "[Your city] tenant union"
- If none exist: **You're the first. That's leverage, not a problem.**

**Can't find crisis data?**
- Local newspaper archive search: "[Your city] housing crisis"
- Council meeting minutes: Search "waiting list" or "mental health"
- MPs' constituency surgeries (they track recurring complaints)

**Informal power brokers unclear?**
- Who donates to the ruling party? (Electoral Commission website)
- Who speaks at council planning meetings? (Meeting minutes)
- Who does the local paper quote as "business leader"?

**Better approximate data now than perfect data never.**

**If you're stuck after 3 hours, STOP researching and START acting.**
