### 1. The Primary Hub: Your Own Website
This is the non-negotiable home base for any white paper.
*   **Dedicated Landing Page:** Companies almost never just link to a PDF. They create a special web page (a "landing page") designed with a single goal: to convince the visitor to download the paper.
*   **Gated Content:** This is the standard practice. The white paper is placed behind a **form** where visitors must provide their name, company, and email address to access it. This is how white papers generate leads.
*   **Website Structure:** It's often found in sections like:
    *   `Resources`
    *   `Insights`
    *   `Research`
    *   `About Us / Our Thinking`
    *   `Solutions` (for a specific product area)

### 2. Third-Party Platforms and Channels
To extend reach far beyond their own website traffic, companies promote white papers through these channels:

*   **Email Marketing:** The white paper is promoted to the company's existing email subscriber list and customer base. It's a key offering in a newsletter.
*   **Social Media:** Companies share the landing page link on LinkedIn, Twitter (X), and other relevant platforms. They often create a series of posts highlighting key statistics or quotes from the paper to drive clicks.
*   **Content Syndication Networks:** Services like **Taboola**, **Outbrain**, or B2B-focused networks pay to promote the white paper on other high-traffic websites (e.g., news sites, industry blogs) as sponsored content or recommended reads.
*   **Industry Publications & Blogs:** Many industry trade magazines or online portals will feature or summarize a strong white paper, often with a link back to the original landing page. Sometimes, this involves a formal content partnership.
*   **Lead Generation Agencies:** Some firms specialize in distributing content to their own curated lists of professionals in specific industries (e.g., IT directors, financial executives) in exchange for a fee per lead.

### 3. Direct Outreach
For high-value targets, the distribution is personal.
*   **Sales Teams:** The sales department uses the white paper as a valuable tool to share with prospects who are encountering the problem the paper addresses. It builds credibility and starts conversations.
*   **Executive Outreach:** A company's leadership might share the paper directly with their peers at other organizations via email or on LinkedIn.

### 4. Academic and Professional Repositories (Less Common)
For white papers that are highly research-oriented and less commercial, they might be uploaded to platforms like:
*   **SSRN (Social Science Research Network)**
*   **arXiv** (for scientific fields)
*   **Academia.edu** or **ResearchGate**
This is done primarily for academic credibility and reach within research communities.

---

### A Crucial Distinction: Publication vs. Promotion

It's important to separate where the paper *lives* from how people *find it*.

1.  **Publication (The Home):** The white paper **lives** permanently on its dedicated landing page on the company's website.
2.  **Promotion (The Pathways):** People **find it** through social media posts, email links, paid ads, search results, and direct sharing. All these pathways should lead back to the landing page on the company's website.
