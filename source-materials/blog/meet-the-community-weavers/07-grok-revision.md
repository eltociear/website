**2. What is a Community Weaver?**
* **Reader-Centered Hook:** If you’re the person who already connects people and gets things moving in your neighborhood, the Community Weaver role turns that invisible work into a supported vocation.
* **Core Definition:** A Community Weaver is **a gardener of the social soil**, fostering trust and collaboration to enable regenerative systems like `Hearts` and `Love Ledger`. Guided by the Social Fabric Framework's Four Pillars (Social Relationships, Connectedness, Common Good, Equality), they facilitate, not manage, aiming for communities so resilient their role becomes unnecessary [cite: 23-grok-revision.md, 28-grok-revision.md].
* **"What it is / isn't" Table:**

| | Community Weaver | Traditional Roles (NGO Worker, Social Worker, Manager) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Focus** | Facilitating community capacity & connection. | Delivering a service or managing a program. |
| **Power Source** | Earned trust and demonstrated reciprocity. | Positional authority and institutional hierarchy. |
| **Goal** | To make themselves unnecessary over time. | To sustain and grow their program or department. |
| **Measure of Success** | Rising LMCI scores and community self-organization. | Program metrics and budget size. |
| **Theoretical Foundation** | Social Fabric’s Four Pillars (Social Relationships, Connectedness, etc.). | Service delivery models. |

**3. Three Core Functions**
* **Function 1: Welcome & Bridge (Accessibility):** Weavers are the human entry point to the GGF ecosystem, using Social Fabric practices like Sovereign Selves to ensure psychological safety. They teach communities to use `Hearts` and `Love Ledger` via low-tech tools like the **Community Care Card** (paper-based gratitude logging), **Heartstarter program** (bootstrapping local projects), **Community Work App** (coordinating teams), and **SMS fallback** (text-based access). For example, a Weaver might use the Heartstarter to launch a Community Provider, ensuring no one is left behind by the digital divide [cite: 15-grok-revision.md, 30-grok-revision.md].
* **Function 2: Guard the Gift (Integrity):** Weavers steward the system's ethics, using Social Fabric’s Generative Conflict practices for restorative mediation. They perform "cultural sincerity checks" for the `Love Ledger`, addressing AI-detected anomalies (e.g., a 'gratitude ring') through non-punitive conversations to realign with Right Relationship principles. This human-tech symbiosis protects the system’s spirit [cite: 15-grok-revision.md, 23-grok-revision.md].
* **Function 3: Weave the Fabric (Connection):** Weavers strengthen relational health—the core of the LMCI—by listening, identifying needs, and convening "Convening Circles" to spark projects (e.g., linking a parent to a Community Work Team, a type of Community Provider [cite: 30-grok-revision (work-in-liberation).md]). Using Social Fabric’s Shared Intent practices, they are the human engine of the LMCI, fostering community resilience [cite: 28-grok-revision.md, 23-grok-revision.md].

**5. Pathway to Becoming a Weaver**
* **The Starting Point:** The journey begins on the `Love Ledger`, where consistent, community-validated care acts serve as a **talent discovery platform**—a living resume identifying those already embodying the Weaver spirit [cite: 15-grok-revision.md].
* **The Training:** A 6-month certification (Work in Liberation Framework) includes:
    * **GGF Systems:** Mechanics of `Hearts`, `Leaves`, and `Love Ledger`.
    * **Facilitation:** Non-coercive communication and restorative conflict resolution.
    * **Cultural Competency:** Indigenous protocols, FPIC 2.0, and local traditions for cultural sensitivity.
    * **LMCI Metrics:** Nurturing drivers of community well-being.
    * **Contextual Training:** Social Fabric’s Stabilization Mode (crisis focus) vs. Regeneration Mode (stable contexts) [cite: 28-grok-revision.md, 23-grok-revision.md].
* **The Compensation:** Weavers earn **AUBI Layer 2 Bonuses** in `Hearts` for validated contributions, ensuring dedication to this vital "Work with Purpose" [cite: 22-grok-revision.md].
* **The Accountability:** Rotational 12-month terms and community feedback via `Love Ledger` prevent capture [cite: 28-grok-revision.md].

**6. Anti-Patterns & Safeguards**
* **Anti-Patterns:** Saviorism, over-centralization, burnout, community gaming, retraumatization, factionalization.
* **Mitigations:** Rotational terms, peer supervision networks, restorative tools, anomaly triage protocol, trauma-informed protocols, and Social Fabric’s bridging tie metrics to counter divides [cite: 23-grok-revision.md].
