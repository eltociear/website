**2. What is a Community Weaver?**
* **Reader-Centered Hook:** If you’re the person who already connects people and gets things moving in your neighborhood, the Community Weaver role turns that invisible work into a supported vocation.
* **Core Definition:** A Community Weaver is **a gardener of the social soil**, fostering trust and collaboration to enable regenerative systems like `Hearts` and `Love Ledger`. Guided by concepts of social relationships, connectedness, common good, and equality, they facilitate, not manage, aiming for communities so resilient their role becomes unnecessary [cite: 23-grok-revision.md, 28-grok-revision.md].
* **"What it is / isn't" Table:**

| | Community Weaver | Traditional Roles (NGO Worker, Social Worker, Manager) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Focus** | Facilitating community capacity & connection. | Delivering a service or managing a program. |
| **Power Source** | Earned trust and demonstrated reciprocity. | Positional authority and institutional hierarchy. |
| **Goal** | To make themselves unnecessary over time. | To sustain and grow their program or department. |
| **Measure of Success** | Rising LMCI scores and community self-organization. | Program metrics and budget size. |

**3. Three Core Functions**
* **Function 1: Welcome & Bridge (Accessibility):** Weavers are the human entry point to the GGF ecosystem, using practices like Sovereign Selves to ensure psychological safety. They teach communities to use `Hearts` and `Love Ledger` via low-tech tools like the **Community Care Card** (paper-based gratitude logging), **Heartstarter program** (bootstrapping local projects), **Community Work App** (coordinating teams), and **SMS fallback** (text-based access). For example, a Weaver might use the Heartstarter to launch a Community Provider, ensuring no one is left behind by the digital divide [cite: 15-grok-revision.md, 30-grok-revision.md].
* **Function 2: Guard the Gift (Integrity):** Weavers steward the system's ethics, using Generative Conflict practices for restorative mediation. They perform "cultural sincerity checks" for the `Love Ledger`, addressing AI-detected anomalies (e.g., a 'gratitude ring') through non-punitive conversations to realign with Right Relationship principles. This human-tech symbiosis protects the system’s spirit [cite: 15-grok-revision.md, 23-grok-revision.md].
* **Function 3: Weave the Fabric (Connection):** Weavers strengthen relational health—the core of the LMCI—by listening, identifying needs, and convening "Convening Circles" to spark projects (e.g., linking a parent to a Community Work Team, a type of Community Provider [cite: 30-grok-revision (work-in-liberation).md]). Using Shared Intent practices, they are the human engine of the LMCI, fostering community resilience [cite: 28-grok-revision.md, 23-grok-revision.md].

**5. Pathway to Becoming a Weaver**
* **The Starting Point:** The journey begins by identifying and resourcing existing "hidden weavers" within a community—those already doing the work without recognition—on the `Love Ledger` itself. A consistent history of authentic, informal care acts—validated by the community's gratitude—serves as a living resume. It's a community recognition tool that identifies individuals who already embody the Weaver's spirit, often before they even recognize it in themselves. Participation is always opt-in, and all data is controlled by the community. The `Love Ledger` is a tool for seeing the good that's already there, not for surveillance [cite: 15-grok-revision.md].
* **The Training:** A 6-month certification (Work in Liberation Framework) includes:
    * **GGF Systems:** Mechanics of `Hearts`, `Leaves`, and `Love Ledger`.
    * **Facilitation:** Non-coercive communication and restorative conflict resolution.
    * **Cultural Competency:** Indigenous protocols, FPIC 2.0, and local traditions for cultural sensitivity. Core modules are mandatorily co-created with local elders or cultural leaders to ensure practices like "Restorative Justice Circles" amplify, rather than replace, existing traditions like kinship-based mediation.
    * **LMCI Metrics:** Nurturing drivers of community well-being.
    * **Contextual Training:** Stabilization Mode (crisis focus) vs. Regeneration Mode (stable contexts) [cite: 28-grok-revision.md, 23-grok-revision.md].
    * **Ethical & Legal Boundaries:** Training on when to hand off issues to licensed therapists or legal mediators, with the GGF providing a framework for professional indemnity.
* **The Compensation:** Weavers earn **AUBI Layer 2 Bonuses** in `Hearts` for validated contributions, ensuring dedication to this vital "Work with Purpose" [cite: 22-grok-revision.md].
* **The Accountability:** To prevent capture, the role is rotational (e.g., 12-month terms), and Weavers are evaluated directly by the community through feedback mechanisms on the `Love Ledger` itself [cite: 28-grok-revision.md]. Introduce a tiered or apprenticeship model for Weavers (e.g., "Apprentice," "Practitioner," "Mentor") to ensure quality control and organic scaling of expertise.

**6. Anti-Patterns & Safeguards**
* **Anti-Patterns:** Saviorism, over-centralization, burnout, community gaming, retraumatization, factionalization.
* **Mitigations:** Rotational terms, peer supervision networks, restorative tools, anomaly triage protocol, trauma-informed protocols, and bridging tie metrics to counter divides [cite: 23-grok-revision.md]. Add: 1) A peer-review network for Weavers to ensure accountability; 2) A community veto mechanism allowing a BAZ council to formally challenge or remove a Weaver; 3) Rotating co-Weavers in larger communities to distribute power.
* **Weaver Support:** Mandatory wellness check-ins, peer supervision circles, and the possibility of funded sabbaticals to ensure the role is sustainable.

**7. Weaving the Fabric: The Relational Foundation**
* **Overview:** The social fabric of trust and cohesion is "Phase Zero" for regenerative systems, rebuilding trust and cohesion as the foundation for `Hearts` and `Love Ledger` [cite: 23-grok-revision.md]. Weavers use concepts like social relationships, connectedness, common good, and equality, along with practices like Sovereign Selves (individual safety) and Generative Conflict (restorative mediation) to prepare communities, whether in stable or crisis contexts.
* **Weaving in a Crisis: Stabilization Mode**  
  In crisis zones, Weavers prioritize immediate life-sustaining actions (e.g., coordinating aid, establishing safe spaces) over metrics, drawing directly from the Social Fabric Framework.
