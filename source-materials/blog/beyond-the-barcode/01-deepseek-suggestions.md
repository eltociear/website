### Suggestions for Improvement & Enhancement

While the outline is already strong, here are a few ideas to make it even more impactful:

**1. Strengthen the Introduction's "Villain":**
*   **Suggestion:** Briefly name the current system's failure. Instead of just "vague marketing terms," you could call it **"greenwashing"** or **"ethics washing."** You could also mention the **"tragedy of the commons"** or **"externalized costs"** as the core economic problems the GGF solves. This gives the intelligent reader a familiar hook to latch onto before you introduce your novel solution.

**2. Add a "Before and After" Table for Skimmers:**
*   **Suggestion:** After the introduction, consider adding a simple, small table. Many readers skim, and a visual comparator is incredibly effective.
    | The Old Way (The Problem) | The GGF Way (The Solution) |
    | :--- | :--- |
    | Burden of proof on the consumer | Burden of proof on the system |
    | Vague, self-reported labels | Verifiable, data-backed scores |
    | Greenwashing is rampant | Transparency is mandatory |
    | Ethical choices are expensive & hard | Regenerative choices are incentivized & easy |
    This reinforces your thesis instantly before you dive into the details.

**3. Flesh Out the "How It Works" with a Concrete Example:**
*   **Suggestion:** In sections 2, 3, and 4, you explain the frameworks well. To make it *sticky*, anchor one of them with a micro-example.
    *   **In Section 2 (Phoenix):** "Imagine the plastic in this water bottle has a passport showing it was previously a medical device, is 100% recyclable back into another bottle, and the company gets `Leaves` for taking it back."
    *   **In Section 4 (DPP):** "Scanning a bag of coffee wouldn't just show a 'Fair Trade' logo. It would show the **Soil Health Score** of the farm it came from, the **average wage** paid to pickers (verified via `Work in Liberation`), and the **circularity score** of the packaging."
    This makes the abstract concept tangible.

*   **Current CTA:** "Explore the frameworks and join us in designing..." is good, but it could be more specific and less daunting.
