**2. What Are 'Leaves'? An Economy for Stewardship**

* **Simple Definition:** "Leaves are a digital token earned by individuals and communities for verified actions that heal and regenerate our natural world."
* **The Core Philosophy:** Explain that Leaves are about **honoring and incentivizing the human labor of stewardship**, not "paying nature." It’s about shifting our economy into a **Right Relationship** with the Earth.
* **Clarifying Value:** "The value of a Leaf isn't arbitrary; it's calibrated by the community based on the effort, skill, and ecological impact of the work, ensuring a fair and meaningful reward for the stewards who perform it."
* **Inclusivity Focus:** "Crucially, Leaves are designed to prioritize and empower those who have been the planet's traditional stewards, creating pathways for Indigenous communities and marginalized groups to be rewarded for their ecological knowledge and labor."
* "To prevent the system from being 'gamed,' the value of Leaves is not just about quantity (e.g., number of trees). It is guided by a **holistic well-being index** that measures real ecological health and community connection. This process is co-designed with Indigenous stewards and local ecologists to ensure we are incentivizing the deep, patient work of regeneration, not just shallow metrics."

**3. How It Works: From Action to Recognition**

* **[Enhancement] A simple numbered list for clarity:**
    1.  **The Regenerative Act:** A person, a co-op, or a community group undertakes a project (e.g., reforesting a hillside in Brazil or establishing an urban community garden in Nairobi).
    2.  **The Verification:** The work is verified by a local, trusted, community-led body. To ensure integrity, this process blends local, on-the-ground knowledge with lightweight technology, like AI-analyzed satellite imagery for larger projects.
    3.  **The Reward:** Once verified, the contribution generates Leaves in the participants' digital wallets. Each Leaf is a unique digital certificate—like an NFT—that acts as a permanent, transparent record of the ecological contribution.
* **Trust Through Transparency: How Verification Works.**
> "Verification isn't based on blind trust; it's a multi-layered process. On the ground, trained and certified **'Community Weavers'**—acting as neutral facilitators—guide the validation process. Their work is transparently logged and overseen by a **global council** that sets standards and audits results. Crucially, an **independent digital justice body** provides a clear pathway to resolve disputes, ensuring the system remains fair and accountable."
