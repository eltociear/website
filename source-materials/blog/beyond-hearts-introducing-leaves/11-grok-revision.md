## 2. What Are 'Leaves'? An Economy for Stewardship

* **Simple Definition:** "Leaves are a digital token earned by individuals and communities for verified actions that heal and regenerate our natural world."
* **The Core Philosophy:** Leaves are about **honoring and incentivizing the human labor of stewardship**, not "paying nature." It’s about shifting our economy into a **Right Relationship** with the Earth.
* **Clarifying Value:** "The value of a Leaf isn't arbitrary; it's calibrated by the community based on the effort, skill, and ecological impact of the work, ensuring a fair and meaningful reward for the stewards who perform it."
* **Inclusivity Focus:** "Crucially, Leaves are designed to prioritize and empower those who have been the planet's traditional stewards, creating pathways for Indigenous communities and marginalized groups to be rewarded for their ecological knowledge and labor."
* **Preventing Shallow Metrics:** "To prevent the system from being 'gamed,' the value of Leaves is not just about quantity (e.g., number of trees). It is guided by a **holistic well-being index** that measures real ecological health and community connection. This process is co-designed with Indigenous stewards and local ecologists to ensure we are incentivizing the deep, patient work of regeneration, not just shallow metrics."

## 3. How It Works: A Step-by-Step Guide

* **1. The Regenerative Act:** A person or group undertakes a project (e.g., reforesting a hillside in Brazil or establishing an urban community garden in Nairobi).
* **2. The Verification:** The work is verified by a local, trusted, community-led body.
    * **Making Verification Concrete:** "This process is designed to be both robust and accessible. For a small community garden, verification might involve the **'Community Weaver'** using geotagged photos and a simple checklist co-signed by garden members. For a large reforestation project, the Weaver might use publicly available satellite data to confirm the work, ensuring claims are credible."
* **3. The Reward:** Once verified, the contribution generates Leaves in the participants' digital wallets. Each Leaf is a unique digital certificate—like an NFT—that acts as a permanent, transparent record of the ecological contribution.
* **The Support System: Keeping It Fair and Accessible**
    > "To ensure this system works for everyone, it’s supported by a simple structure. Trained **'Community Weavers'** act as on-the-ground guides to help people get started. A **global non-profit** provides open-source tools and standards to ensure quality. And for any disagreements, a transparent, **independent arbitration service** is available. This isn't about creating a new bureaucracy, but providing the light-touch support needed to keep the system trustworthy."

## 6. The Bridge to the Real World: How It All Connects

* "This is where the rubber meets the road. Hearts and Leaves are not 'play money'; they are designed with a robust bridge to the real-world economy."
* **A Simple Walk-Through: From a Leaf to a Dollar**
    > "Let's follow the journey:
    > 1. **Earning Leaves:** Maria’s youth group earns **100 Leaves** for restoring a local riverbank. The value was determined by the local Stewardship Council, based on the project's ecological impact and the effort involved.
    > 2. **Converting to Hearts:** Through a simple app (the 'Value Exchange'), Maria converts the 100 Leaves into **100 Hearts**. The initial 1-to-1 exchange rate is designed for stability and simplicity.
    > 3. **Using Hearts in the Community:** The group uses their 100 Hearts to pay for a celebratory meal at Fatima's Cafe, a local business that accepts Hearts.
    > 4. **Redeeming for Dollars:** At the end of the week, Fatima redeems the 100 Hearts for **$100** from the local **Community Treasury**. This treasury is a professionally managed fund, backed by global commons funds and run in partnership with local credit unions, guaranteeing that every Heart has a stable, real-world value."
