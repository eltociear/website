To your crucial question about the GGF's role, I would strongly recommend the **hybrid approach**.

### **Strategic Recommendation: A Hybrid Approach**

* **Diverging from the GGF** would weaken the book. Its core principles would feel less grounded, and you would lose the unique, systemic "secret sauce" that makes your approach truly revolutionary.
* **Making the GGF key** from page one would likely alienate a broad audience. It would require readers to buy into a massive, complex system before they've even understood the core value proposition of regenerative investing.

The **hybrid strategy** is the most powerful. The book should stand on its own as a definitive guide to the *philosophy and practice* of regenerative capital allocation. It then introduces the **GGF as the most advanced, fully-integrated "operating system"** for putting these principles into practice at scale. This allows the book to serve two functions: it's a valuable, standalone guide for any investor, *and* it's the perfect "gateway drug" to the entire GGF ecosystem.
