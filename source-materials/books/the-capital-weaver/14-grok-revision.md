# Book Outline: "The Capital Weaver"
***A Practical Guide to Regenerative Investing and the Dawn of a New Economy***

**Core Purpose:** To deliver a compelling, accessible, and practical guide to regenerative capital allocation that stands alone as a transformative resource for investors while serving as a gateway to the Global Governance Frameworks (GGF) ecosystem. The book introduces the "Capital Weaver" archetype and provides tools to operationalize this vision, culminating in an invitation to engage with the GGF as the ultimate regenerative operating system.

---

### **Front Matter**

* **Quick Reference Guide:** One-page table summarizing the Five Principles, Scorecard categories, key GGF frameworks (e.g., **AUBI**, **BAZs**, **Hearthstone**), and page references for Explorer, Practitioner, and Architect Tracks. Includes links to a digital companion (e.g., **Venture Commons** app with RoR Calculator, Scorecard Worksheet, Investment Memo Template).  
* **How to Use This Book:** Explains the three-part structure (Principles, Practice, GGF), the role of Sarah’s narrative for relatability, the purpose of persona icons (Philanthropist, Impact Investor, Community Fund, Family Office) for tailored guidance, and the “Deal Labs” as practical case studies to bridge theory and application. Recommends starting with Part I for novices, Part II for practitioners, or Appendix K for GGF enthusiasts.

### **Preface: The Investor's Dilemma**

* **The Hook:** Sarah, a seasoned investor, faces cognitive dissonance: her portfolio delivers strong ROI but fuels systemic crises—displaced communities, degraded ecosystems, eroded trust. She seeks to align her capital with a thriving world, reflecting the polycrisis driving these failures.  
* **The Stakes:** The polycrisis demands a new capital allocation model. The GGF offers a regenerative economy where investments heal, delivering resilient returns as an emergent property of systemic well-being.  
* **The Reader Promise:**  
  - *Philanthropists*: Fund systemic change with lasting impact.  
  - *Impact Investors*: Balance profit and regeneration in patient capital deals.  
  - *Community Funds*: Build resilient, polycentric local economies.  
  - *Family Offices/Wealth Advisors*: Craft legacies that steward the commons.  
  This book equips you to become a "Capital Weaver," with practical tools and a phased path to the GGF, supported by early adopters like [hypothetical firms] gaining client trust and regulatory favor (e.g., EU Taxonomy alignment).  
* **Community Connection:** Introduces the **Capital Weavers Community of Practice** as a peer network for sharing insights and deal flow, accessible via **Venture Commons**.

* **The Diagnosis:** The polycrisis—climate disruption, social inequity, economic fragility—stems from capital allocation prioritizing short-term profit. ESG and impact investing are "painkillers" within an extractive paradigm.  
* **The Shift:** From sustainability to regeneration, resonating with global traditions like Indigenous commons and African circular economies. The "Capital Weaver" mends social, ecological, and economic fabrics.  
* **The Book’s Role:** A definitive guide to regenerative investing, offering principles, tools, and a phased introduction to the GGF, with a primer in **Appendix K: The GGF at a Glance**.  
* **Sarah’s Voice:** Sarah questions how to align her portfolio with her values, setting up her journey.
