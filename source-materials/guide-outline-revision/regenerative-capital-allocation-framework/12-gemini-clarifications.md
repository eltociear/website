### **1. Addressing Dr. Aliyah Khan: The "Institutional Investor" Upgrade**

Dr. Khan's critique is that the framework is operationally unworkable for at-scale capital. To address this, we must build clear bridges to the existing financial world.

**Proposed Changes:**

* **Reframe the RoR as a Complementary Metric, Not a Replacement:**
    * In **Part 1**, clarify Principle 4: "The **Return on Regeneration (RoR)** is a normalized index designed to run *alongside*, not replace, traditional financial metrics like IRR. It provides the missing social and ecological data needed for a truly comprehensive risk-adjusted return analysis."
    * In **Appendix E**, the RoR specification should show how the final score can be presented alongside IRR, not blended into it (e.g., "IRR: 8%, RoR: 7.2/10").

* **Create a "Light-Touch" Due Diligence Pathway:**
    * In **Part 2**, introduce a two-stage due diligence process. **Stage 1 (Screening)** would use readily available data and self-reported metrics. Only high-potential investments that pass this initial screen would proceed to **Stage 2 (Deep Diligence)**, which involves the more resource-intensive on-the-ground assessments.

* **Professionalize and Decentralize Assurance:**
    * In **Appendix E (The RoR & Assurance Protocol)**, revise the assurance model. Instead of relying solely on BAZ councils, the protocol should establish a **certified ecosystem of third-party verifiers**, accredited by the **Regeneration Audit Council**. This creates a professional, scalable system, much like LEED certification in green building.

* **Elevate the Standards Crosswalk:**
    * Move the **"Standards Crosswalk"** from Appendix G into the main body of **Part 2 (The Regenerative Capital Scorecard)**. Frame it as: "The GGF Scorecard is designed for deep alignment. Here is how its categories map directly to major global reporting standards like CSRD and ISSB, allowing for seamless integration into your existing workflows."
