### **Clarifications for Grok: Enhancing the Discovery Commons (v0.3)**

#### **1. Radical Equity: Decolonizing Research Power Structures**

* **Action:** In Section 3, amend the **Composition** of the `council_global_research`.
* **Clarification:** Mandate that **50% of leadership roles must be held by researchers from the Global South**. Furthermore, specify that the council's secretariat and annual summit must rotate between Global North and Global South institutions to dismantle geographic power centers.
* **Action:** In Section 3, under `mechanism_research_equity_fund`, add a new funding source.
* **Clarification:** Introduce the **`mechanism_knowledge_debt_levy`**. This is a reparative levy on historically dominant research nations, with the revenue directly capitalizing the `mechanism_research_equity_fund`.
* **Action:** In Section 3, amend the `protocol_open_science`.
* **Clarification:** Add a **Language Justice** clause requiring all research abstracts and summaries to be published in English and at least one other major Global South language (e.g., Swahili, Hindi, Spanish, Arabic), with AI-powered translation tools funded by the commons.

#### **2. Open Science on Steroids**

* **Action:** In Section 3, amend the `protocol_open_science`.
* **Clarification:** Add two new mandates:
    1.  **Pre-Registration Mandate:** All research proposals must pre-register their hypotheses and methodologies on the Discovery Commons platform *before* funding is released.
    2.  **Open Peer Review:** All peer review will be conducted openly on a blockchain-backed system, with reviewers' contributions permanently and publicly linked to the publication.
* **Action:** In Section 3, amend the `protocol_indigenous_research_sovereignty`.
* **Clarification:** Enhance this protocol with **"Dynamic Consent."** This means Indigenous communities have the technical ability (via APIs managed through the `framework_digital_commons`) to modify or retract consent for the use of their data in real-time.

#### **3. Disrupting Intellectual Property**

* **Action:** In Section 3, amend the `protocol_ethical_ip`.
* **Clarification:**
    * **Patent Abolition:** Explicitly state that patents are abolished for research in critical domains funded by the commons, including climate technology, pandemic response, and AI safety.
    * **Planetary Commons License:** Introduce the **`protocol_planetary_commons_license`** as the default for all other research. This license allows free use and derivation but requires commercial users to pay a tiered percentage of profits back to the `institution_public_research_fund`.
    * **AI Transparency Clause:** Mandate that any AI model trained on Commons data must be open-sourced and contributed back to the commons.

#### **4. Crisis-Driven Innovation**

* **Action:** In Section 5, under the `protocol_crisis_research_response`, add two new mechanisms.
* **Clarification:**
    1.  **Viral Priority-Setting:** During a crisis, citizen assemblies are empowered to directly allocate 10-20% of the emergency research fund via real-time voting.
    2.  **`institution_crisis_labs` (Crisis Labs):** These are temporary, decentralized "open-source war rooms" that bring together scientists, engineers, and local communities to rapidly co-develop solutions, with fast-tracked funding and automatic IP waivers.

#### **5. Gamifying Collaboration**

* **Action:** In Section 3, add a new mechanism: **`mechanism_impact_credits`**.
* **Clarification:** These are non-monetary tokens awarded for pro-social academic behavior (e.g., cross-regional collaboration, mentoring Global South colleagues, high-quality peer review). These credits can be redeemed for benefits like conference funding or sabbaticals.
* **Action:** In Section 4, add a new program under the `institution_public_research_fund`: **"Global South Moonshot Challenges."**
* **Clarification:** This program allocates 10% of the research fund to high-risk, high-reward challenges with solutions judged by grassroots panels.

#### **6. Accountability Shockwaves**

* **Action:** In Section 5, under "Compliance & Enforcement," add two new tools.
* **Clarification:**
    1.  **Algorithmic Auditing:** The `council_global_research` will use AI to monitor the commons for "knowledge hoarding" (e.g., systemic gatekeeping of data) and can automatically trigger sanctions.
    2.  **Whistleblower Bounties:** A formal policy offering a percentage of recouped funds to whistleblowers who expose misconduct.

#### **7. Wildcard: A Science Shadow UN (Modification)**

* **Agreement & Modification:** The idea of a parallel, democratically-elected body is brilliant but a full override power could be destabilizing. We can achieve a similar outcome by integrating it with existing GGF patterns.
* **Action:** In Section 3, create a new institution: **`institution_global_science_assembly`**.
* **Clarification:** This is a directly elected body of scientists and citizens (e.g., 1 million votes = 1 seat). It does not have direct override power. Instead, like the `Council of Guardians` in the `framework_treaty`, it has two critical functions:
    1.  It can initiate **binding public inquiries** into the research priorities of the `council_global_research`.
    2.  With a 2/3 majority vote, it can **force a supermajority reconsideration vote** within the `council_global_research` on any decision deemed to neglect existential risks. This is a powerful check and balance without creating a rival government.

#### **8. Implementation Tweaks**

* **Action:** In Section 4, under the `institution_regional_research_hubs` mandate, add a **"Subsidiarity Veto."**
* **Clarification:** Regional Hubs have the authority to veto global research priorities if they can demonstrate a direct conflict with urgent, life-sustaining local needs (e.g., water scarcity research taking precedence over theoretical physics).
