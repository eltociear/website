### **Answers to Claude's Outstanding Questions**

**1. Economic Modeling & Sustainability**
* **What % of current global R&D spending does this require?**
    * The framework would initially be capitalized by a target of **5-10% of the public R&D spending** of signatory nations, supplemented by the `mechanism_knowledge_debt_levy` and allocations from the `mechanism_gcf`. The long-term goal is to become the primary channel for the majority of public R&D investment.
* **How do you handle free-rider nations?**
    * This is addressed through strategic incentivization and enforcement. Nations that do not contribute will not have access to the benefits of the `protocol_planetary_commons_license` for their commercial sectors, will be subject to trade restrictions under the `framework_gaian_trade` for knowledge-intensive goods, and their institutions will be ineligible for the `mechanism_institutional_recognition` program.
* **What is the pathway for transitioning from the current patent system?**
    * The transition is managed through the **`protocol_ip_transition`** (see clarification below). It protects existing IP portfolios while creating strong incentives (e.g., priority funding access, `mechanism_impact_credits`) for researchers and institutions to voluntarily place new discoveries into the commons.

**2. Stakeholder Engagement Strategy**
* **What makes Harvard, Oxford, Tsinghua want to join?**
    * A combination of prestige, access, and influence. They would gain priority access to massive, globally coordinated funding streams (`institution_public_research_fund`), a seat at the table in setting the global research agenda (`council_global_research`), and the ability to attract top global talent who want to work within the open, mission-oriented commons. The **"Discovery Commons Certified"** status will become a global benchmark for top-tier research institutions.
* **How do corporate researchers (Google, Pfizer) participate?**
    * They can participate through public-private partnerships. If they use Commons-funded data or tools, they are bound by the **`protocol_planetary_commons_license`**, requiring them to contribute a percentage of profits back to the fund and open-source any derivative technologies. This creates a symbiotic relationship.

**3. Legal Framework Integration**
* **How does this relate to existing science cooperation treaties?**
    * The Discovery Commons, established under the authority of the `framework_treaty`, is designed to supersede and integrate previous, fragmented treaties. It provides a single, coherent, and enforceable global framework that fulfills and expands upon the cooperative ambitions of earlier agreements.
* **How do you protect researchers with existing patent portfolios?**
    * The **`protocol_ip_transition`** includes a "grandfather clause" that protects patents filed before an institution joins the Commons. However, to receive Commons funding for new research, institutions must agree to place all *new* discoveries under the Commons licensing model.
