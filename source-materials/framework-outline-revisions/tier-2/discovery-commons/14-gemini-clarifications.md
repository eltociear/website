### **Answers to Claude's Outstanding Questions**

You asked how the framework handles four critical challenges. The iterative revisions have successfully integrated answers for each:

1.  **How does this coordinate with existing international frameworks like the Paris Agreement or ILO Just Transition guidelines?**
    * **This is answered in Section 7 of the v1.0 draft.** The `council_global_transition` is explicitly mandated to liaise with the UNFCCC (for Paris Agreement alignment) and the ILO to ensure GGF protocols complement and enhance, rather than conflict with, existing international law and norms.

2.  **What happens with multinational corporations that operate across multiple jurisdictions with different timelines?**
    * **This is answered in Section 7.** Under the authority of the `framework_treaty`, the `institution_dj_tribunal` has jurisdiction over corporate entities operating within any signatory nation. Crucially, it is mandated to assess a corporation's compliance *holistically across its global operations*. This prevents the "shell game" of shifting harmful activities to non-compliant jurisdictions.

3.  **How do you handle critical industries (like rare earth mining) that are harmful but currently essential for renewable energy infrastructure?**
    * **This is answered in Section 7.** The "Protocol for Critical Transition Industries" creates a special designation for these sectors. They are subject to the Sundown Protocol but are granted extended, carefully managed timelines. A key condition is their mandatory, verifiable investment in developing circular economy alternatives and less harmful extraction methods, a process overseen by the **Technology Integration Hub**.

4.  **What are the protocols for reversible decisions if unexpected technological breakthroughs make certain industries suddenly viable again?**
    * **This is answered in Section 7.** The "Protocol for Re-evaluation" allows the `council_planetary_health` to initiate a formal review if a certified technological breakthrough fundamentally alters an industry's impact score (BHI). However, the protocol includes extremely high safeguards: any reversal requires the explicit consent of both the `institution_gya` (Global Youth Assembly) and the relevant BAZ councils, ensuring the decision is held to the highest standard of intergenerational and ecological justice.
