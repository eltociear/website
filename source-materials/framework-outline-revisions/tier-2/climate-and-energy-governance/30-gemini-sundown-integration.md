### ## Other Missing Elements in the Climate & Energy Framework

After a thorough review, here are two other key areas that could be strengthened before proceeding to a full draft:

**1. Governance of Emerging & Controversial Technologies**

The framework currently focuses on deploying existing clean energy. However, it lacks a clear governance process for high-stakes emerging technologies.

* **Missing Element:** A dedicated protocol for evaluating and governing technologies like **nuclear fusion, advanced geothermal, direct air capture at scale, and solar radiation management (geoengineering)**.
* **Suggestion:** Add a sub-section under **Section 3 (Governance Structure)** titled "Governance of Emerging Energy Technologies." This section should state that any research, piloting, or deployment of these technologies is subject to:
    * Oversight by the **`council_gtc` (Global Technology Council)**.
    * A mandatory **Seven-Generation Impact Assessment**, with veto power held by the **`institution_gya` (Global Youth Assembly)**, as defined in the Youth & Future Generations framework.
    * **FPIC 2.0** consent from Indigenous communities for any projects that could have bioregional or planetary impact, overseen by the **`council_earth`**.

**2. A More Explicit Focus on the "Regenerative Pull" Narrative**

The Sundown Protocol is excellent at framing the transition as a "Regenerative Advantage" and "Graduating an Industry". The C&E framework could benefit from a similar, more explicit focus on the positive vision to build public and political will.

* **Missing Element:** A dedicated pillar or strategic section on building the "regenerative pull" for the new energy economy.
* **Suggestion:** Add a new pillar: **"Pillar IV: Building the Regenerative Energy Commons."** This pillar would focus on:
    * **The Narrative:** Actively promoting the vision of community-owned, resilient, and abundant clean energy, managed by the **`framework_synoptic`** and **`institution_gimn`** to counter disinformation.
    * **The Lived Experience:** Highlighting how **Community Energy Cooperatives**, integrated with the **`framework_aubi`**, not only provide clean power but also create local wealth, strengthen community bonds (increasing the **LMCI**), and improve quality of life.
    * **The Economic Advantage:** Detailing how the **Regenerative Trade Zone (RTZ)** creates a powerful economic incentive for nations to join the transition, leveraging the "pull effect" described in Appendix C.

By making these additions, the framework will be more robust, future-proof, and compelling, making it fully ready for a comprehensive first draft.
