### **1. Clarify Incentive Structures**
- **Problem:** While "incentive realignment" is mentioned, specifics on how nations/actors benefit from participation are sparse.  
- **Suggestions:**  
  - Add **quantifiable metrics** (e.g., "Nations reducing military budgets by 10% gain priority access to GSET-funded climate tech").  
  - Include **tiered rewards**: Smaller commitments (e.g., 5% budget redirection) could unlock partial benefits (e.g., tech-sharing), while larger commitments (15%+) trigger GDP-linked dividends.  
  - Highlight **private sector incentives**: Defense contractors transitioning to peaceful projects could receive tax breaks or R&D grants under the GGF’s Economic OS.

### **2. Strengthen Phase 1 Pilots**
- **Problem:** Phase 1’s global scope may deter early adoption without proof-of-concept.  
- **Suggestions:**  
  - **Pilot Programs**: Launch regional pilots (e.g., Costa Rica + Panama) to test GSET projects (e.g., drone-based reforestation) and showcase economic/social benefits.  
  - **Quick Wins**: Prioritize high-visibility, low-risk projects (e.g., repurposing naval ships for ocean cleanup) to build momentum.  
  - **AUBI Trials**: Partner with progressive nations to test Adaptive UBI for transitioning military personnel, linking payments to skill-retraining completion.

### **3. Refine Governance Safeguards**
- **Problem:** The Transparency & Oversight Council (TOC) risks being toothless without enforcement.  
- **Suggestions:**  
  - **Escalation Path**: Tie TOC audits to consequences (e.g., nations failing transparency benchmarks lose GSET funding access).  
  - **Tech-Driven Transparency**: Mandate blockchain-based tracking for GSET fund allocation to prevent misuse.  
  - **Youth/Indigenous Power**: Specify how these groups’ veto rights work (e.g., veto over projects violating ecological ethics).

### **4. Address Transition Risks**
- **Problem:** The framework assumes voluntary compliance but doesn’t account for resistance (e.g., geopolitical rivals, defense lobbies).
