### **The Core Strategy: Convert Lists to Prose with "The Rule of Three"**

The most effective way to improve readability is to move away from long bulleted lists and embrace a more narrative paragraph structure. A powerful technique for this is what I'll call **"The Rule of Three."**

Instead of a long list of bullet points, for each major idea, you will:

1.  **Write a strong opening paragraph:** This paragraph introduces the core concept and explains *why* it's important. It sets the stage.
2.  **Select the 2-3 most powerful examples:** From your list of bullet points, choose the two or three most illustrative and impactful examples. Dedicate a short paragraph or a clear sub-section to each. This makes the concept concrete and memorable without overwhelming the reader.
3.  **Write a concluding/transitional paragraph:** This paragraph summarizes the key takeaway from the examples and smoothly links to the next major idea.
