### ## 2. Addressing the Template Library

The success of the framework hinges on the quality of its practical tools. We will create an appendix with starter templates.

#### **Actionable Plan:**

Create a new appendix section titled `### Appendix B: Core Implementation Template Library`. This section will provide initial drafts of the three key templates.

* **Template 1: Pilot Feedback Template:**
    * **Sections:** 1) Pilot Details, 2) Key Successes, 3) Unexpected Challenges, 4) Core Learnings, 5) Suggested Adaptations, 6) Community Story (a qualitative narrative of the experience).

* **Template 2: Autonomy Statement:**
    * **Sections:** 1) Core Principle Alignment (how the pilot adheres to GGF principles), 2) Proposed Adaptation (the specific tool or process being changed), 3) Rationale for Adaptation (why this is necessary for the local cultural/ecological context), 4) Anticipated Impact (how the change will improve outcomes).

* **Template 3: Adaptation Report:**
    * **Sections:** 1) Summary of Pilot & Feedback, 2) The Adaptation Implemented (referencing the Autonomy Statement), 3) Measured Outcomes (using the Implementation Health Dashboard KPIs), 4) Lessons for the Community of Practice, 5) Next Steps (e.g., continue pilot, scale, or pause).

### ## 3. Addressing the Community Weaver Curriculum

The Community Weaver is the most critical human role in the ecosystem. Outlining their training is essential.

#### **Actionable Plan:**

Create a new appendix section titled `### Appendix C: Community Weaver Certification Curriculum Outline`. This section will detail the training modules required for certification.

* **Module 1: GGF Core & Ethics:**
    * **Content:** The Triumvirate, Core Principles, the Moral Operating System, and the non-negotiable role of the Indigenous & Traditional Knowledge Governance Framework.

* **Module 2: The Implementation Framework in Practice:**
    * **Content:** Deep training in facilitating Stage 0 Bootstrapping, the Context Assessment Rubric, and the MEL Cycle.

* **Module 3: The Facilitator's Craft:**
    * **Content:** Practical skills in the four core competencies: Cultural Translation, Trauma-Informed Facilitation, Low-Tech & Analog Tool Implementation, and Conflict Resolution.

* **Module 4: The GGF Toolkit:**
    * **Content:** Hands-on training with the core GGF "Tool Stacks" (e.g., BAZ Starter Kit, AUBI Pilot Guide).

* **Certification Requirement:** Completion of all modules plus a mentored apprenticeship, co-facilitating one full implementation pilot with a senior Weaver.
