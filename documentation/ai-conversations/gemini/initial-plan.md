You have hit the nail on the head. This is the classic "Chicken and Egg" problem of every startup: *You need money to hire the person who gets the money.*

Here is the strategic answer: **You need "Bridge Funding" (Bryggfinansiering).**

You are correct that the *Verksamhetsledare* will be the one writing the big applications (Vinnova, EU, etc.) to fund their own full-time salary later. But you cannot expect a professional to work for free for 3-6 months while waiting for those decisions.

### The Financial Plan (The "Bridge")

You need a small pot of money **now** (Q1) to pay this person for a limited "Förstudie" (Pre-study).

1. **The Source:** This initial seed money almost certainly needs to come from **your father** (or a very fast private grant). Public money (Vinnova) is too slow for a January start.
2. **The Amount:** You don't need a full salary. You need a "Consultant Fee" (Konsultarvode) for a fixed deliverable.
* *Estimate:* **50,000 – 75,000 SEK.**
* *What this buys:* Someone working ~20% (1 day a week) for 3-4 months.


3. **The Deliverable:** Their job for that 75k is to:
* Set up the legal entity (Förening).
* Write and submit 2-3 major funding applications.
* Secure the "Letter of Intent" from a municipality.



### How to pitch this to your Father

When you send the email/document tomorrow, you need to be specific about this "Bridge." He will understand this term.

**Add this logic to your conversation with him:**

> *"Pappa, strategin är att rekrytera en 'Verksamhetsledare' som kan dra in de stora projektpengarna från Vinnova/EU.
> Men jag kan inte anställa någon på 'spekulation'. Jag behöver en **såddfinansiering (seed funding)** på ca 75 000 kr.
> Detta täcker ett konsultarvode under Q1 för att göra jobbet med ansökningarna. Om vi får beviljat projektstöd tar det över lönekostnaden sedan. Om vi inte får det, avslutas uppdraget. Det är en begränsad risk för att testa om konceptet flyger."*

### Summary of the Workflow

1. **Step 1 (Now):** You ask Father for **Seed Money (e.g., 75k SEK)**.
2. **Step 2 (Jan):** You use that money to sign a **Consultancy Contract** with the Verksamhetsledare (not employment yet).
* *Contract says:* "You get 25k/month for 3 months to secure long-term funding."


3. **Step 3 (Feb-Mar):** The Verksamhetsledare **writes applications** to Vinnova/Kulturrådet/Kommuner.
4. **Step 4 (Jun-Aug):** Decision comes.
* *Scenario A (Success):* You get 1M SEK grant -> You hire them full time.
* *Scenario B (Fail):* Money runs out -> You pause and rethink, having only risked the seed money.



**Does this make sense?** It reduces the risk for everyone. You aren't promising a salary you can't pay; you are buying a specific "service" (fundraising) to get the ball rolling.
