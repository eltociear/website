Global Governance Frameworks
Björn Kenneth Holmström

1. The Strategic Horizon

"We are not trying to repair the nation-state system. We are building the parallel infrastructure that allows humanity to migrate—structurally and psychologically—from a system of extraction to a system of stewardship."

This document outlines the 25-year strategic roadmap for the Global Governance Frameworks (GGF). It moves beyond the immediate pilot phase (2026) to visualize the emergence of a planetary network of Bioregional Autonomous Zones (BAZs).

Our goal is not utopian. It is an engineering response to the "Polycrisis" (Climate, AI, Geopolitics). As legacy systems strain under biophysical limits, GGF provides the "Lifeboats" that eventually connect to form the new mainland.

Phase I: The Seed & The Sovereign
2026 – 2028

The Objective: Proof of Concept in two distinct typologies.

In this phase, we act locally but design globally. We are proving that the architecture works in the real world.

1. The Urban Pilot (Stockholm/Uppsala)

  • Focus: "Regionens Nervsystem" (The Nervous System).
  • Goal: Validate the Hearts currency protocol within a high-tech, municipal context.
  • Outcome: A working digital ledger measuring care flows (psychological resilience) in public infrastructure. The "Tech Proof."

2. The Indigenous Pilot (Sápmi)

  • Focus: The first true Bioregional Autonomous Zone (BAZ).
  • Goal: Establish a governance treaty where "Land" has veto power, solving the conflict between industrial "Green Energy" and cultural survival.
  • Outcome: A model of sovereignty that bypasses the Nation-State. The "Political Proof."

Phase II: The Mycelial Network
2028 – 2032

The Objective: Inter-Bioregional Trade & The "Shadow UN."

Once the Stockholm and Sápmi nodes are active, we connect them. We begin to invite other "edge" regions—Scotland (Wellbeing Economy), Cascadia (Pacific NW), and Estonia (Digital State)—to adopt the framework.

The League of Bioregions

We launch a transnational alliance that functions not as a debating club (like the UN), but as a Trade Network.

  • Hearts Exchange: A mechanism allowing care value generated in Sápmi (ecosystem services) to be traded for technical value generated in Stockholm or Estonia.
  • Ecocide Firewall: The network enforces the "Treaty for Our Only Home" through code. Corporations or regions violating planetary boundaries are algorithmically excluded from the Hearts trade network.

Phase III: The Great Migration
2032 – 2045

The Objective: Institutional Transfer & Capacity Absorption.

This period corresponds to the predicted intensification of the Polycrisis (resource scarcity, climate displacement, AI disruption). The Nation-State system will likely face solvency crises and inability to deliver basic services.

  • The "Lifeboat" Function: GGF infrastructure (BAZs) begins to absorb functions the state sheds: elder care, food security, local energy grids.
  • Employment Guarantee: As AI automation creates mass technological unemployment, the Hearts economy absorbs millions into the "Regeneration Corps" (restoring forests, wetlands, and community fabric).
  • The Shift: People don't "overthrow" the government; they simply stop relying on it. Legitimacy transfers to the bioregional governance structures because they actually work.

Phase IV: The Open Hand
2045 – 2050

The Objective: Planetary Stewardship.

By 2050, the "League of Bioregions" has effectively superseded the Westphalian Nation-State as the primary unit of human coordination.

  • The End of War: War requires the accumulation of capital and energy surplus by centralized entities. In a distributed bioregional network with circular economies, the "payoff matrix" for war becomes negative.
  • Planetary Homeostasis: Governance is no longer about "growth" but about "metabolic balance" with the biosphere.
  • The Answer to the Child: We can finally answer the question "Why is there war?" with: "There used to be, before we learned to design differently."

"The closed fist is a reflex of fear. The open hand is a deliberate choice.
This architecture makes that choice sustainable."
